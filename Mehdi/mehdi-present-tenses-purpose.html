Reading Text
Simon Sinek is a British-American author and speaker. He works✅ Present simple — his permanent job / what he does in general with leaders and organisations around the world. Every year, he gives✅ Present simple — a repeated habit / "every year" is a signal word hundreds of talks and writes✅ Present simple — a general, ongoing truth about his life about leadership, trust, and purpose.

His most famous idea is simple: people don't buy✅ Present simple — a universal truth / a fact that is always true what you do, they buy why you do it. He believes✅ Present simple — "believe" is a state verb — it almost NEVER uses the continuous that great leaders start✅ Present simple — a general principle, a truth he describes with their purpose, not their product.

Right now, Sinek is focusing✅ Present continuous — "right now" signals something happening at this moment in time on the idea of infinite thinking in business. He is writing✅ Present continuous — an activity in progress, not yet finished new content and is collaborating✅ Present continuous — a temporary project happening these days with organisations that want to build long-term cultures.

Many coaches use✅ Present simple — a general habit / many coaches do this regularly his frameworks with their clients. In coaching, the "Start With Why" question helps✅ Present simple — a permanent truth / it always helps, not just now clients find clarity about their purpose — exactly what a good coach does✅ Present simple — describes the general role of a coach.
💡 Notice the pattern: works, gives, believes = present simple (habits, facts, permanent truths). is focusing, is writing, is collaborating = present continuous (happening right now, in progress, temporary).

Grammar · 10 min
The Two Tenses
Same verb, different meaning. The form tells you when and how often something happens.
Present Simple Habits, facts, permanent situations, universal truths
I / You / We / They + verb     He / She / It + verb + s
Habit / Routine
Something you do regularly
"He gives talks every year."
Fact / Truth
Always true — not just now
"People buy the why, not the what."
Permanent situation
How things are in general
"He works with leaders worldwide."
State verbs ⚠️
know / believe / want / need / understand — almost never continuous
"He believes in purpose." ✓
Signal words → Present Simple
always usually often sometimes never every day / week / year in general
Present Continuous Actions in progress now, temporary situations, trends
Subject + am / is / are + verb + -ing
Right now
Happening at this exact moment
"He is writing new content."
These days
A temporary situation around now
"I am working on a new project."
Trend / change
Something that is changing or developing
"More people are choosing coaching."
Temporary ≠ permanent
It will not last forever
"She is running a free workshop."
Signal words → Present Continuous
right now at the moment these days currently today this week / month still
Key Contrast Same verb — completely different meaning
Present Simple
He works with leaders.
→ His job in general
I coach entrepreneurs.
→ What I do for a living
She reads every morning.
→ A daily habit
Present Continuous
He is working with a client.
→ Right now, this session
I am coaching a new client.
→ This week / these days
She is reading Sinek's book.
→ Currently, not finished yet
⚠️ State verbs — never continuous: know · believe · want · need · understand · like · love · hate · prefer · own · seem · appear

Say "I know what you mean" — never "I am knowing what you mean" ✗

Exercise B · Choose the right sentence
1. Which sentence is correct?
💡 "Know" is a state verb — it never uses the continuous.
2. A coach is describing what they do for a living. Which is correct?
💡 Describing your job in general = present simple.
3. Which sentence talks about something happening right now?
💡 "Right now" needs present continuous.
4. Which describes a current trend?
💡 "These days" + a changing trend = present continuous.

3. "Every morning, she is reading for one hour."
Hint: "every morning" — a regular habit
Free Production · 10 min
Talk About Yourself
Answer these questions about your own life and work. Use present simple for habits and facts — present continuous for what is happening these days. Fluency first.
Speaking Prompts
1
What do you do every day as a coach? Describe your typical routine.
→ Present simple — habits and routines
2
What are you working on right now — this week or this month?
→ Present continuous — what is in progress these days
3
Like Sinek, what do you believe about coaching? What is your "why"?
→ Present simple — state verbs: believe, think, know, feel
4
Is anything changing in your business right now — new clients, new ideas, new direction?
→ Present continuous — trends and temporary changes
