Reading Text · Word of Mouth vs. Digital Marketing
Once you know your value, the next question is: how do you reachreach = contact, get to / reach your audience = find the people you want to talk to the right people?

Word of mouth is one of the oldest and most powerfulNotice the comparative structure: "one of the most powerful" = superlative. We'll study this next lesson! forms of marketing. When a satisfied client recommendsrecommend = suggest to someone else / recommander your services to a friend, that recommendation is more crediblecredible = believable, trustworthy / "more credible" = comparative of credible than any advertisement. It builds trusttrust = confiance — one of the most important words in coaching and marketing faster, and it costs nothing. However, it is also slower to scalescale = grow bigger / slower to scale = takes more time to reach large numbers of people. You depend on other people to spread your message.

Digital marketing — through social media, a website, or email — is faster and widerfaster and wider = two comparatives joined with "and" — common in business English. You can reach hundreds of people with one post. But it requires more time, more consistency, and a clear messageclear message = a simple, focused communication — no jargon, no confusion. Without that, digital marketing is less effective"less effective" = the negative comparative — less + adjective (no change to the adjective) than simply talking to people directly.

For a coach like Mehdi, the most effective strategy"most effective" = superlative. Grammar · 8 min
Comparatives
How to compare two things in English. Three rules — clear, fast, with business examples.
📐 The Comparative — 3 Rules
Rule 1 · Short adjectives (1 syllable) → add -er
adjective + -er + than
Examples fast → faster than  ·  cheap → cheaper than  ·  clear → clearer than
Business "Word of mouth is cheaper than digital advertising."
⚠️ Spelling big → bigger (double the consonant)  ·  nice → nicer (drop the e)
Rule 2 · Long adjectives (2+ syllables) → more / less + adjective
more / less + adjective + than
Examples effective → more effective than  ·  credible → more credible than
Business "A recommendation is more credible than an advertisement."
⚠️ Never more faster ✗  ·  more bigger ✗ — short adjectives never use "more"
🇫🇷 French link more = plus  ·  less = moins  ·  than = que
Rule 3 · Irregular forms — learn these by heart
good
better than
bad
worse than
far
farther / further
little
less than
Business "Building trust is better than running ads."
💡 Quick test: which rule for each?  expensive → ?  ·  fast → ?  ·  good → ?
Answers: more expensive · faster · better
