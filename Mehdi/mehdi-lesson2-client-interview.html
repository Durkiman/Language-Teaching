Your Client Questionnaire · In English
🗂️ These are questions you ask your clients at the start of coaching. Today you'll practise saying them in English.
Part 1 · Personal Profile
How would you describe yourselfUse: "How would you describe…?" — polite, open question professionally, and what values guide"What values guide your choices?" — guide = direct, shape your choices?

Are you more comfortable"Are you more comfortable with…?" — great for preference questions with the technical side, the process side, or the long-term strategic vision?

How do you react"How do you react when…?" — react = respond, behave in a situation when things don't go as planned — do you speed up, put things offput things off = procrastinate (more natural in spoken English), or take a step back?

What are your main strengthsstrengths = things you do well; opposite: weaknesses? For example: communication, leadership, creativity.

What are your main weaknessesweaknesses = areas to improve. Often asked as: "What do you find difficult?"? For example: impatience, difficulty delegating.
Part 2 · Entrepreneurial Vision
Why do you want to start your own business"start your own business" = create / launch an enterprise right now?

What do you hope to achieve"What do you hope to achieve?" — hope to = want to / aim to with this project?

How would you measure your success"How would you measure…?" — conditional for hypothetical, polite questions? For example: revenue, client satisfaction, work-life balance.

What could discouragediscourage = make someone lose motivation; could = possibility question or exhaust you in this process?
Part 3 · Skills & Organisation
What key skills do you already have"What skills do you already have?" — already = at this moment, before starting? For example: sales, marketing, team management.

What would you prefer to delegatedelegate = give a task to someone else to do quickly?

How do you make decisions"make a decision" NOT "take a decision" — common learner error when things are uncertain?
💡 Notice the question patterns: "How do you…?", "What are your…?", "How would you…?" — these are the three main structures you'll practise today.

Free Production · 15 min
You're the Coach
Time to run a real interview — in English. Your teacher plays the client.
🎭 Roleplay Scenario

You are Mehdi, an entrepreneurial coach. Your client is Karim — he wants to start his own sustainable architecture firm.

Your goal: interview Karim using your questionnaire questions in English. Ask at least 6 questions. Listen to his answers and ask one follow-up question.

Remember: fluency first — don't stop if you make a mistake. Keep going like a real professional conversation.

Your Question Bank · Use these to run the interview
1
How would you describe yourself professionally?
→ Open the interview warmly. Smile, maintain eye contact.
2
What values guide your choices?
→ Follow up: "Can you give me an example?"
3
Are you more comfortable with the technical side or the strategic vision?
→ Good for understanding the client's profile type
4
How do you react when things don't go as planned?
→ Listen for: do they accelerate, step back, or freeze?
5
What are your main strengths and weaknesses?
→ Core coaching question — give them time to think
6
Why do you want to start your own business right now?
→ Look for real motivation vs. impulse
7
What do you hope to achieve with this project?
→ Then ask: "And how would you measure that success?"
8
What could discourage you along the way?
→ Important for coaching — understand their risk zones
