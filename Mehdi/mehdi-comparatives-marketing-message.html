Real Example · Wellness Coaching Brand Copy
Are you living in your head — or living in your life?

Most people spend their days managing stressmanage stress = deal with stress day to day (not eliminate it) rather than understanding it. They push through, stay busy, and hope things will get better. They rarely do — not without the right tools.

At Breathe Forward, we believe that the most powerful transformation"the most powerful" = superlative of "powerful" — today's grammar focus! starts with the simplest thing: your breath. Our breathwork coaching helps you reconnect with your bodyreconnect = come back to a connection you lost / se reconnecter, release what you've been carrying, and find a sustainable calmsustainable = that lasts, that you can maintain / durable that actually lasts.

This isn't the fastest fix"the fastest" = superlative of "fast" — another one! Notice how superlatives make marketing more confident.. But it is the deepest one"the deepest" = superlative of "deep" — short adjective, add -est.

Our clients don't just feel better — they understand whyThis is the same structure from Pillar 1! "They don't just… — they…" You already know this pattern.. They leave with a personal practice, real insight, and the confidence to keep goingconfidence = confiance — essential in coaching and marketing language — even when things get hard.
💡 Notice: The text uses "the most powerful", "the fastest", and "the deepest". These are superlatives — the grammar for today. They make marketing copy sound confident and clear.

Grammar · 8 min
Superlatives
Last lesson you learned comparatives. Today: superlatives — when something is the best, the strongest, the most. Read each rule, then do the drag exercise underneath it.
Rule 1 Short adjectives (1 syllable) → the + adjective + -est
the adjective -est
fast → the fastest deep → the deepest clear → the clearest big → the biggest ⚠️ double consonant
"This is the fastest way to build trust with a client."
🎯 Drag each word into the correct slot to build the superlative sentence.
Breathwork is form of bodywork.
the most deepest deeper
Rule 2 Long adjectives (2+ syllables) → the most / the least + adjective
the most adjective
effective → the most effective powerful → the most powerful ❌ the most fastest  ·  ❌ the most biggest
"Word of mouth is the most powerful tool a coach has."
🎯 Drag the words into the right order.
A clear message is part of your marketing.
the most important more
Rule 3 Irregular forms — must be memorised
good
→
better than

the best

→

→

"The best marketing is a client who tells their story."
🎯 Complete the sentence with the correct irregular superlative.

🔁 Comparatives vs. Superlatives — at a glance
Adjective Comparative Superlative
fast faster than the fastest
deep deeper than the deepest
effective more effective than the most effective
powerful more powerful than the most powerful
good better than the best
bad worse than the worst

Guided Writing · 12 min
Write Durim's Marketing Message
Use the sentence frames to write a short marketing message for the breathwork practice. Speak each sentence aloud before you write it. Use at least one superlative.
🧘 Remember: You're writing for Durim's breathwork practice. You know this business — you interviewed him in Lesson 2. Use what you know. Think from the client's perspective.
① Opening Hook — 1 sentence
The Hook
A strong marketing message starts with a question or a bold statement that the reader recognises.
Do you feel ?
→ Use a feeling your client has. Make them say "yes, that's me."
② The Problem — 1–2 sentences
Problem
Most people
→ Describe the problem your client has — in their words, not yours.
Consequence
Without support, they
→ What happens if they do nothing?
③ The Solution — 1–2 sentences
What you do
Through breathwork, I help clients
→ Use a verb from today's vocabulary: reconnect / release / build awareness / carry less…
How it's different
This is approach to
→ Use a superlative here — make it confident.
④ The Outcome — 1–2 sentences
Result
As a result, my clients
→ Concrete, measurable, real. What do they tell you after?
Closing line
They don't just feel better —
→ Same powerful structure from Pillar 1. End strong.
