Input · 10 min
The Uncomfortable Truth About Selling
Read this text carefully — out loud if possible. It gives you the language and ideas you'll need to coach your client today. Tap highlighted phrases to explore them.
Reading Text
For many people who work in wellness, coaching, or creative fields, selling feels wrongfeel wrong = seem inappropriate, uncomfortable — sembler faux / mal à l'aise. It feels like they are putting a priceput a price on = assign a monetary value to something — mettre un prix sur on something that should be about genuine connectiongenuine connection = a real, authentic relationship — connexion authentique and transformation, not money.

But this resistance to sellingresistance = the feeling of pushing back, refusing / résistance often comes from a misunderstandingmisunderstanding = a wrong idea about something — malentendu / idée fausse. Selling is not about convincing someone to buy something they don't needA common fear — but good selling is the opposite: it helps people access what they genuinely need.. It is about helping them see the valuesee the value = understand what they will gain / réaliser la valeur of something they already want.

When a practitioner struggles to chargestruggle to charge = find it hard to ask for money / avoir du mal à facturer for their work, it is often because they don't fully believedon't fully believe = aren't completely convinced — ne croient pas entièrement in the value they offer. Or they fear rejectionfear rejection = are afraid the person will say no — peur du rejet — and confuse a "no" to their price with a "no" to them as a person.

The shiftshift = a change in thinking or perspective — changement, glissement happens when you realise: not selling is also a choicePowerful reframe — staying silent or undercharging is an active choice, not neutrality.. When you don't offer your work clearly, the person who needs it never finds itnever finds it = doesn't get the help they need — ne le trouve jamais. Selling, then, is not about you. It is a service to the clientservice to the client = an act of helping them, not an act of self-promotion.
💡 Key idea for today's coaching session: Selling = helping someone see the value of what they already want. Not convincing. Not pushing. Serving.

Coaching Session · 20 min
Mehdi Coaches Durim
You are the coach. Durim is your client. He is building his breathwork and teaching business — and today, the topic is his relationship with selling. Run the session in English.
🎭 Session Setup
Your client: Durim — breathwork practitioner and language teacher, based in Brooklyn. He is starting to build his business more seriously but struggles to talk about money and sell his services confidently.
Your goal as coach: Help him explore his relationship with selling. Understand where the discomfort comes from. Help him see it differently — without telling him what to think.
Mehdi · Coach Phase 1 — Open the Session (5 min)
Start with open questions. Make Durim feel at ease. Let him talk — don't fill the silence.
  • ?
    How do you feel about selling your work — breathwork, teaching, coaching?Open question — no right answer. Just listen.
  • ?
    When did you last have a conversation where someone asked about your prices? What happened?Concrete, recent — brings it to life.
  • ?
    On a scale of 1 to 10, how comfortable are you asking someone to invest in your services?A number forces clarity. Then ask: "Why that number?"
Durim · Client Possible Responses — Teacher Plays These
Response to Q1
"Honestly, it feels uncomfortable. I believe in what I do — but asking for money feels like I'm reducing it to a transaction. It doesn't feel right."
Response to Q2
"Last week, someone asked me about a private breathwork session. When they asked the price, I hesitated and said 'it depends'… I didn't even give a clear number."
Response to Q3
"Maybe a 4 or 5. I know my work has value — but when I say the price out loud, I always feel like they're going to say it's too expensive."
💡 Teacher tip: Respond naturally — don't just read the script. Add a hesitation, a pause, a real emotion. Make him work for the information.
Mehdi · Coach Phase 2 — Go Deeper (8 min)
Now dig into the why. Use what he said. Reflect it back. Ask questions that help him see it differently.
  • ?
    You said it feels like a transaction. Can you tell me more about that? What does "transaction" mean to you in this context?Reflect his own word back to him — powerful coaching move.
  • ?
    When you hesitated and said "it depends" — what were you afraid of?Go to the fear directly. Don't soften it.
  • ?
    If a friend told you they felt uncomfortable charging for their work — what would you say to them?Classic reframe — shifts perspective from inside to outside.
  • ?
    Do you think not selling your work clearly is fair to the people who need it?The text's key idea — selling is a service. Let him arrive at it himself.
Durim · Client Possible Responses — Phase 2
On "transaction"
"I think… when money enters the conversation, it changes something. It becomes about the price, not the experience. And I don't want people to feel like they're just buying a product."
On the fear
"I was afraid they'd say no. And somehow… a 'no' to my price feels like a 'no' to me. Like they don't value what I do."
On the reframe
"That's a good question. If a friend said that, I'd probably tell them they're undervaluing themselves… Hmm. I guess I do the same thing."
Mehdi · Coach Phase 3 — Land the Insight (5 min)
Don't give him the answer — help him find it. One or two powerful questions, then let him sit with it.
  • ?
    Based on what you just said — what do you think is the real reason you hesitate?He often already knows. Give him space to say it.
  • ?
    What would change if you truly believed your work was worth what you charge?Hypothetical — opens the door to a different reality.
  • ?
    What is one small thing you could do differently next time someone asks about your prices?Always end with a concrete, actionable insight.
Notes Teacher's Language Log — During the Session
Note errors to address after — not during. Keep the session flowing.
💡 After the session: Pick the 1–2 most recurring errors. Name them once, specifically. Then move to reformulation — that's where he fixes them.
Reformulation · 10 min
Hear It Right
Three rounds. Mehdi speaks — teacher reflects back corrected. Mehdi repeats. This is how errors become habits — not through correction, but through hearing the right version in context.
The Method
1
Mehdi speaks for 60 seconds on the prompt — no stopping, no self-correcting.
2
Teacher reformulates — naturally, warmly: "So what you're saying is…" or "If I understand you correctly…" — with all errors silently corrected.
3
Mehdi repeats the teacher's version out loud — with conviction, not mechanically. Then we go again.
Round 1
"Tell me what you discovered about yourself in the coaching session today. What was the most important insight for you?"
💡 This reflects on the content of the session — he's speaking from real experience, which means real vocabulary, real grammar challenges.
Round 2
"As a coach — what did you notice about Durim's relationship with selling? What do you think is really holding him back?"
💡 Switches perspective — he's now analysing you as a client. More complex sentences, more opinion language.
Round 3
"Now think about yourself — do you have the same problem with selling your own coaching? How do you feel about it?"
💡 The hardest round — personal and vulnerable. Longest silences, most authentic language. Give him time.
