Presentation · 12 min
What is Value Creation?
Read the text carefully. Tap any highlighted phrase to see what it means.
Reading Text · ~150 words
Every successful business starts with one question: what problem do you solve?The core of value creation — what pain or need does your business address?

Value creationvalue creation = producing something useful that people are willing to pay for means giving your clients something they truly need — something that makes their life easierA simple, powerful way to describe your value: "I make my clients' lives easier by…", saves them time, or helps them grow.

As a coach, your value is not just your knowledge. It is your ability to help someone see clearlyhelp someone see clearly = help them understand their situation better when they feel lost, to identify their strengthsidentify = find, recognise / strengths = what someone does well, and to build a plan that works for them specifically.

The key question your client is always asking — even if they don't say it out loud — is: "what's in it for me?"What's in it for me?" = What do I gain? Why should I care? Very common in business English."

Your job as a coach is to answer that question before they even ask it.Proactively = before being asked. Shows confidence and client-focus. This is what separates a trusted advisortrusted advisor = someone clients rely on for honest, expert guidance from just another service provider.
💡 Key idea: Value is not what you offer — it is what your client receives. Always think from the client's perspective.

Free Production · 15 min
You're the Coach
Your teacher plays a real client — a breathwork practitioner. Interview them to understand their value creation.
🧘 Your Client Today

Name: Durim  ·  Practice: Breathwork & Somatic Coaching

Durim runs a breathwork practice. He helps people regulate stress, reconnect with their body, and unlock emotional blocks through conscious breathing techniques. He has been practising for several years but has never clearly defined his business value — he describes what he does, not what his clients gain.

Your goal as coach: understand how Durim currently creates value — and help him see it more clearly in English.

Your Interview Questions — Use at Least 6
1
How would you describe what you do — and the problem you solve?
→ Listen carefully: does he describe actions or outcomes?
2
Who is your ideal client — what are their problems, habits, and fears?
→ From his questionnaire — key for value creation
3
What do your clients gain from working with you?
→ Reframe if needed: "From their perspective, what changes?"
4
Are you more comfortable with the technical side of breathwork, or the results it creates for clients?
→ Helps locate where his perceived value sits
5
Why should a client choose you rather than another breathwork practitioner?
→ His differentiation — what makes him unique?
6
How do you measure success with a client — how do you know you added value?
→ Pushes him to think in outcomes, not processes
7
What's the biggest transformation you've seen in a client?
→ Great follow-up — real stories reveal real value
8
What do you think your clients value most about working with you?
→ Final reflective question — links back to "What's in it for me?"

That's interesting… Can you tell me more? What do you mean by…? So, from your client's perspective… If I understand correctly… Can you give me an example? What value does that create? What's the outcome for the client? Let me ask you something else… That makes a lot of sense.
