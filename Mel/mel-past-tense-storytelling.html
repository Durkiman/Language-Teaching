📖 The past tense
0:00
Why this matters to you

Right now you can say what you feel and what you want. But you can't tell a story yet. You can't say I went, I saw, it was beautiful. Every real conversation with Durim's family will involve the past — where you've been, what you ate, how it was. Today you unlock that.

The 6 verbs you need right now

These are the most common verbs in any story or memory. Learn these and you can already tell a lot.

Shkova
present: shkoj → I go
Dje shkova në qytet.
I went
Je suis allé(e)
Pashë
present: shoh → I see
Pashë diçka të bukur.
I saw
J'ai vu
Hëngra
present: ha → I eat
Hëngra byrek sonte.
I ate
J'ai mangé
Piva
present: pi → I drink
Piva kafe me nanën.
I drank
J'ai bu
Isha
present: jam → I am
Isha shumë e emocionuar.
I was
J'étais
Ishte
present: asht → it is
Ishte shumë e bukur.
It was / She was / He was
C'était / Elle était / Il était
The pattern — how most verbs go past
Verbs ending in -oj in the present become -ova in the past:

punoj → punova · lexoj → lexova · mësoj → mësova

Think: 🇫🇷 -ai in French (je parlai, j'achetai) — same principle, just a different sound.

The 6 verbs above are irregular — just memorise them. They're the most frequent in any real conversation.
Full conjugation — shkova (I went)

Focus on shkova (I) and shkove (you) — those are the ones you'll use most.

The big unlock — past + feelings together

You already know feeling words. Now combine them with the past. Read each sentence aloud:

Shkova Kosovë dhe isha shumë e emocionuar.
I went to Kosovo and I was very excited.
Pashë familjen e tij dhe ndihem e lumtur.
I saw his family and I feel happy.
Hëngra byrek dhe ishte e shijshme.
I ate byrek and it was delicious.
📄 Reading
0:00
How to read this

Read the text below once for meaning. Then read it again and notice every verb highlighted in green — those are all past tense forms you just learned. After reading, answer the questions without looking back.

Një ditë në Prishtinë — A day in Pristina
Pramë, shkova Prishtinë me Durimin për herën e parë. Isha shumë e emocionuar — dhe pak e nervozme gjithashtu.

Kur mbërritëm në qytet, shkuam direkt te shtëpia e familjes. Nana e Durimit hapi derën dhe buzëqeshi shumë. Ishte shumë e ngrohtë dhe e mirëpritur.

Ne hëngëm bashkë — hëngra byrek, tavë kosi, dhe shumë fruta. Piva kafe turke për herën e parë — ishte e fortë por e shijshme.

Pasdite, shkova me Durimin në shëtitoren e Prishtinës. Pashë shumë njerëz, kafene, muzikë rrugore. Ishte gjallë dhe plot energji.

Natën, isha e lodhur por shumë e lumtur. Mendova: "Du me ardhë sërish."
Vocabulary from the text
mbërritëm we arrived
shkuam we went
hapi she opened
buzëqeshi she smiled
hëngëm we ate
mendova I thought
shëtitorja the promenade
gjallë lively, alive
Comprehension — answer in Albanian if you can, English if you need to
Notice the pattern

Count the green verbs in the text — there are 13. You already know most of them: shkova, isha, ishte, hëngra, piva, pashë.

The new ones follow the same pattern — mendova (I thought, from mëndoj), shkuam / hëngëm (we forms). Same verb, different person ending.
