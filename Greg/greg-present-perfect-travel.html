Read & Listen

Sam's Trip to Brazil

Copacabana beach, Rio de Janeiro

Read the text. Notice the two highlighted colours — they're different for a reason.

I've travelled to a lot of places over the years, but Brazil is still my favourite.

Two years ago, I went to Rio de Janeiro with a friend. We stayed near Copacabana beach and we watched the sunrise from Sugarloaf Mountain on our first morning. I even tried a caipirinha for the first time — it's the national cocktail, made with a spirit called cachaça.

I've also been to São Paulo and Salvador. I've never been to the Amazon though — that's still on my list. Have you ever heard live samba music? It's completely different when you hear it in person.

In 2023, I went back for Carnival. It was one of the best experiences of my life — we danced in the street for three days straight.

If you're planning a trip to Brazil, I'd suggest learning a few words of Portuguese first — people really appreciate it. It's worth asking locals where they actually eat, not just the tourist spots.
Present Perfect — experience, no specific time
Past Simple — specific time, finished story

Answer out loud:

  • Where did Sam go two years ago?
  • Which Brazilian cities has Sam been to?
  • What happened in 2023?
  • What does Sam suggest before visiting Brazil?
⏱ 8 min
The rule

Two ways to talk about the past

Present Perfect

have/has + past participle

Experience. We don't say exactly when.

I've been to Spain.
Have you ever tried sushi?

Past Simple

verb + -ed / irregular

A specific moment. We say when.

I went to Spain in 2022.
I tried sushi last week.

Quick test: Is there a specific time word (last year, in 2019, yesterday)? → Past Simple. Talking about life experience in general? → Present Perfect.
Time words

Signal words

Present Perfect

ever, never, already, yet, so far, in my life

Past Simple

yesterday, last year, in 2019, when I was 20, ago
