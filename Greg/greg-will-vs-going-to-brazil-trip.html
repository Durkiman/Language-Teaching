Planning a First Trip to Brazil

Copacabana beach, Rio de Janeiro

This text is based on real travel-planning guides. Notice the two highlighted forms — they're both about the future, but they work differently.

Planning a first trip to Brazil isn't simple — the country is huge, so most travellers start by choosing between the cities and the wild nature. I'm going to focus on Rio and the coast this time, since two weeks isn't enough to see everything.

Timing matters too. Rio's hottest months are December to March, so I'm not going to travel then — it's also Carnival season, and prices triple. I'll probably go in September or October instead, when it's quieter and cheaper.

For getting around, domestic flights connect the major cities, and it's usually cheaper to book early. I'm going to book my flights about three months ahead. As for safety, most guides say the same thing: stay in busy areas, don't flash valuables, and use ride apps instead of hailing taxis on the street. I'll definitely follow that advice.

There's still a lot I haven't decided. I'll probably try feijoada and a caipirinha, but I'm not sure about the Amazon yet — I might add a few extra days if the budget allows it. One thing's for sure: I'm going to learn a few words of Portuguese before I go.
Going to — plan already decided
Will — prediction / not fully decided

Adapted from Brazil travel-planning guides (Rough Guides, Extraordinary Journeys, Nomadic Matt).

⏱ 8 min
The rule

Two ways to talk about the future

Going to

am/is/are + going to + verb

A plan or intention — already decided before now.

I'm going to book my flights next week.

Will

will + verb

A prediction, a spontaneous decision, or something not fully certain.

I'll probably visit the Amazon too.

Quick test: Has the decision already been made? → Going to. Deciding right now, or guessing? → Will.
Signal words

Words that hint at each form

Going to

I've already booked… / My plan is… / I've decided…

Will

probably, maybe, I think, I'm not sure, I might
