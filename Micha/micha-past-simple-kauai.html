Part 1 Fast Facts

Location
Kauai is one of 8 Hawaiian islands in the North Pacific Ocean — about 4,000 km from mainland USA.
🌿
Nickname
Called "The Garden Isle" because it is the greenest, most lush island in Hawaii — covered in jungle!
🌧️
Wettest Place
Mount Waialeale on Kauai is one of the wettest places on Earth — it rains almost every single day!
🦜
Wildlife
Kauai is home to thousands of wild chickens, rare seabirds, and the endangered Hawaiian monk seal.
🎬
Famous For
Many Hollywood movies were filmed here — including Jurassic Park and Pirates of the Caribbean!
🏔️
Nā Pali Coast
A dramatic coastline of sharp green cliffs rising 1,200 metres straight from the sea — breathtaking!

Part 3 Reading
🎤 Text A — Teacher reads aloud · Micha listens
The Island That Hollywood Loves
Kauai is the oldest of the eight Hawaiian islands. Millions of years ago, a volcano erupted under the Pacific Ocean, and over time, rock and lava built up until an island appeared above the surface of the water.

Today, Kauai is one of the most beautiful places on Earth. The island receives so much rain that its mountains are permanently covered in thick jungle. The ancient Hawaiians called it "Kaua'i" — a name that means a calm place after a storm.

The island's most spectacular landscape is the Nā Pali Coast — a series of enormous green cliffs that rose straight out of the ocean. No road was ever built along this coast because the cliffs are too steep. The only way to see it is by boat, helicopter, or a long, difficult hike.

Film directors fell in love with Kauai because it looked so wild and untouched. Steven Spielberg chose it for Jurassic Park in 1993, and it became the island of dinosaurs that the world saw on screen. Since then, more than 70 Hollywood films have been shot here.
📖 Text B — Micha reads
Waimea Canyon — The Grand Canyon of the Pacific
On the west side of Kauai, there is a giant canyon called Waimea Canyon. It is about 16 km long and 900 metres deep.

Mark Twain, a famous American writer, visited Hawaii in the 1800s. He saw the canyon and called it "The Grand Canyon of the Pacific". The colours are incredible — red, orange, brown, and green all together.

The canyon formed because an old volcano collapsed and rain carved the rock for millions of years. Today, a small river runs through the bottom, and wild goats live on the steep walls.

Every year, thousands of tourists come to Kauai to hike the canyon trails. They take photos, eat picnics on the edge, and sometimes see a rainbow after the rain. It is one of the most visited places in all of Hawaii.
