Two Ways to Talk About Animals

We can talk about what an animal always does (a fact) — or what it is doing right now (happening this second). English uses two different grammar forms for this.

On peut parler de ce qu'un animal fait toujours (un fait) — ou de ce qu'il est en train de faire maintenant. L'anglais utilise deux formes grammaticales différentes pour ça.

Right Now"The elephant is drinking water."
Always True"Elephants drink a lot of water every day."

📐 Present Continuous — Happening Right Now

Le présent continu — ce qui se passe maintenant

subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
SubjectFormExample
Iam + -ingI am watching the lions.
He / She / Itis + -ingThe elephant is drinking water.
You / We / Theyare + -ingThe zebras are running.

Je regarde... / L'éléphant boit... (en ce moment) / Les zèbres courent...

📐 Present Simple — Always True

Le présent simple — toujours vrai

subject + verb  |  He/She/It + verb + -s
SubjectFormExample
I / You / We / TheyverbLions live in groups.
He / She / Itverb + sAn elephant eats plants all day.
💡 Tip: Present Simple = a fact that's always true. Present Continuous = happening right now, in this moment.

Présent simple = un fait toujours vrai. Présent continu = qui se passe maintenant, à cet instant.

⚠️ Common mistakes:

❌ "The lion sleeping." → ✅ "The lion is sleeping." (never forget am/is/are!)

❌ "Elephants is drinking water every day." → ✅ "Elephants drink water every day." (general fact = Present Simple)

❌ "The zebra runs right now." → ✅ "The zebra is running right now." (happening now = Present Continuous)

N'oublie jamais am/is/are · Fait général → présent simple · En ce moment → présent continu

📝 Reading — At the Waterhole

Lecture — Au point d'eau

Right now, three elephants are drinking water at the waterhole. Elephants drink a huge amount of water every day — up to 200 liters! A zebra is standing near the elephants, watching them carefully. Zebras live in large herds and they always stay close together for safety. Suddenly, a bird lands on the elephant's back — birds often do this to eat insects.
