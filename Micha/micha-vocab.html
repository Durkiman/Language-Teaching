Past Simple
Actions finished in the past — regular & irregular
›
Use Past Simple for actions that started and finished in the past.
🇫🇷 Comme le passé composé en français — actions terminées dans le passé.
TypeFormulaExample🇫🇷
PositiveSubject + past verbHavertz scored in the 6th minute.Havertz a marqué à la 6e.
NegativeSubject + didn't + BASEArsenal didn't win the trophy.Arsenal n'a pas gagné.
QuestionDid + subject + BASE?Did Saka score?Est-ce que Saka a marqué ?
Regular verbs — add -ED
play → played · score → scored · miss → missed · tackle → tackled
Les verbes réguliers ajoutent -ed au passé
Irregular verbs — must learn by heart!
go → went · win → won · see → saw · make → made · hit → hit
Les verbes irréguliers changent de forme — à mémoriser !
⚠️ Classic mistakes
"He didn't scored" → "He didn't score"After didn't — always BASE form
"Did he won?" → "Did he win?"After Did — always BASE form
🏷️
Possessives
Adjectives (my/your…) and pronouns (mine/yours…)
›
Possessive adjectives go BEFORE a noun. Possessive pronouns stand ALONE — no noun after.
🇫🇷 Adjectifs possessifs avant un nom. Pronoms possessifs seuls, sans nom.
PersonAdjectivePronoun🇫🇷
Imyminemon/ma — le mien/la mienne
Youyouryourston/ta — le tien/la tienne
Hehishisson/sa (à lui) — le sien
Sheherhersson/sa (à elle) — le sien
Weouroursnotre/nos — le nôtre
Theytheirtheirsleur/leurs — le leur
⚠️ Classic mistakes
"This is theirs trophy" → "This is their trophy"Theirs = pronoun, no noun after. Use their before a noun.
"The trophy is their" → "The trophy is theirs"Standing alone with no noun → use pronoun form.

Comparatives
Comparing 2 things — bigger than, more beautiful than
›
Comparatives compare 2 things. Always use "than" after.
🇫🇷 Le comparatif compare 2 choses — toujours "que" après l'adjectif.
TypeRuleExample🇫🇷
Short (1 syllable)add -er than
CVC → double last letter		bigger · hotter · deeper · greenerplus grand · plus chaud · plus profond
Ending -ychange to -ier thanprettier · rockierplus joli
Long (2+ syllables)more + adj + thanmore beautiful · more volcanicplus beau · plus volcanique
Irregularmemorise!better · worse · furthermeilleur · pire · plus loin
⚠️ Classic mistakes
"more bigger than" → "bigger than"Never "more" AND "-er" together
"bigger Belgium" → "bigger than Belgium"Always need "than" when comparing
"more far" → "further"Irregular: far → further → the furthest

Superlatives
The most extreme — the biggest, the best, the most beautiful
›
Superlatives = the most extreme in a group. Always use "the" before.
🇫🇷 Toujours "le/la plus ___" — et toujours "the" en anglais.
TypeRuleExample🇫🇷
Shortthe + adj-estthe biggest · the hottest · the deepestle plus grand · le plus profond
Ending -ythe + adj-iestthe prettiestle plus joli
Longthe most + adjthe most beautiful · the most volcanicle plus beau · le plus volcanique
Irregularmemorise!the best · the worst · the furthestle meilleur · le pire · le plus loin
⚠️ Classic mistakes
"the most biggest" → "the biggest"Never "the most" AND "-est" together
"the most good" → "the best"Irregular: good → better → the best

"Going to" Future
Plans & predictions — is going to, are going to…
›
Use for plans already decided and predictions based on evidence.
🇫🇷 Pour des plans décidés et des prédictions basées sur des indices.
TypeFormulaExample🇫🇷
Positiveam/is/are going to + BASESpain is going to win.L'Espagne va gagner.
Negativeam/is/are NOT going to + BASEArsenal is not going to win.Arsenal ne va pas gagner.
QuestionIs/Are + subject + going to + BASE?Is France going to qualify?Est-ce que la France va se qualifier ?
Am / Is / Are — which one?
I am · He/She/It is · We/You/They are going to…
Je vais · Il/Elle va · Nous/Vous/Ils vont
⚠️ Classic mistakes
"Spain is going to wins" → "Spain is going to win"After "going to" — always BASE form
"They going to play" → "They are going to play"Can't drop "are" — am/is/are is always required
