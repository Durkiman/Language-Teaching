Ferrari World opened in Abu Dhabi in 2010 and immediately became famous for one reason: Formula Rossa — the fastest roller coaster on Earth. It was built to feel like driving a real Formula 1 car. The ride reaches 240 km/h in just 4.9 seconds.

Riders must wear goggles because the wind is so strong it could damage their eyes. The park sits under the world's largest roof — a giant red shape inspired by the Ferrari logo.

"It doesn't feel like a ride. It feels like being fired out of a cannon." — a visitor

Six Flags Great Adventure holds a terrifying record: it is home to Kingda Ka — the tallest roller coaster ever built. At 139 metres, it is taller than the Statue of Liberty. The ride shoots passengers straight up at 206 km/h, then drops them almost vertically back down.

The whole ride lasts just 28 seconds — but passengers often reported that it felt like the longest 28 seconds of their lives. The park also has a drive-through safari with 1,200 wild animals — lions, tigers, and elephants!

"I looked down and saw the whole park below me. Then I screamed." — a 14-year-old visitor

Universal Studios Japan is not just a theme park — it is a step inside a movie. The park opened in Osaka in 2001 and since then grew into one of the most creative places on Earth. It has a full Harry Potter world with Hogwarts Castle, a Super Nintendo World where you can play Mario Kart in real life, and a giant Minions zone.

In 2021, they added Super Nintendo World — the first ever real-life video game world. Visitors wear a special wristband that connects to the attractions. They can collect coins, fight bosses, and earn points — just like in a real game!

"Walking into Hogwarts felt more real than watching the films." — a Harry Potter fan
