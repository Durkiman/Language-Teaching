🧠 Retrieval — Possessives + Saint-Barths
1
Give the possessive adjective AND pronoun for: I · she · they
Donne l'adjectif possessif ET le pronom possessif pour : I (je) · she (elle) · they (ils)
I → my (mon/ma) · mine (le mien/la mienne)  |  she → her (son/sa) · hers (le sien)  |  they → their (leur) · theirs (le leur)
2
What's wrong with these sentences? Fix them.
a) "This is mine book."   b) "The trophy is my."
Qu'est-ce qui ne va pas dans ces phrases ? Corrige-les.
a) "This is my book." — mine ne peut pas précéder un nom / mine cannot come before a noun
b) "The trophy is mine." — my doit être suivi d'un nom / my must be followed by a noun
3
Which country owned Saint-Barths from 1784 to 1878?
Quel pays possédait Saint-Barths de 1784 à 1878 ?
Sweden / La Suède. France sold it in exchange for trading rights in Gothenburg (Göteborg). The capital was renamed Gustavia after King Gustav III.

⚡ THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
🔵
"This is my island." — my = before a noun ✅
C'est mon île. — "my" est un adjectif, il précède TOUJOURS un nom.
🟡
"This island is mine." — mine = replaces the noun ✅
Cette île est la mienne. — "mine" est un pronom, il remplace "my island".
❌
"This is mine island" ← FAUX! / WRONG!    "This island is my" ← FAUX! / WRONG!
🇫🇷 ASTUCE POUR MICHA — Pour se souvenir :
🔵 Adjectif = "mon / ma / mes / ton / son / notre / leur" → suivi d'un nom
🟡 Pronom = "le mien / le tien / le sien / le nôtre / le leur" → seul, sans nom après
💡 En anglais : si le mot suit "is/are" et qu'il n'y a pas de nom après → c'est un pronom (mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs)

03 📖 Reading — Teacher reads aloud 5 MIN
🎤 Teacher reads · Micha listens · Spot the possessives!
The Island That Belongs to Everyone — and No One
Saba is tiny, wild, and unlike any other island in the Caribbean. Its capital is called "The Bottom" — not because it is low, but because the Dutch word bodem means "ground". Its streets are narrow, its houses are white with red roofs, and its people are proud of their extraordinary home.

The island belongs to the Netherlands, but Saba has always felt like a place apart. Its volcanic peak — Mount Scenery — is the highest point in the entire Dutch kingdom. When clouds wrap around its summit, the mountain looks like something from a fantasy world.

Durim is here now. His feet are on its volcanic rock. The view from his window is his alone — the turquoise sea, the green hills, the quiet streets below. This lesson is ours.

05 ✍️ Write — Saba Sentences 5 MIN
🌿 Write 4–5 sentences about Saba using possessives
// Click a starter to begin — use both 🔵 adjectives and 🟡 pronouns
🔵 Saba is a tiny island. Its capital is called ___
Saba est une petite île. Sa capitale s'appelle ___ — [adjectif possessif "its" = son/sa pour une chose]
🔵 The island belongs to the Netherlands, but its people are proud of their culture.
L'île appartient aux Pays-Bas, mais ses habitants sont fiers de leur culture. [deux adjectifs possessifs]
🟡 Durim visited the island. The view from his window was ___ — it was all his.
Durim a visité l'île. La vue depuis sa fenêtre était ___ — c'était tout à lui. [adjectif "his" puis pronom "his"]
🟡 The island has its own unique style. The red rooftops are theirs — nobody else has them.
L'île a son propre style unique. Les toits rouges sont les leurs — personne d'autre ne les a. [adjectif "its" puis pronom "theirs"]
