📖 THE STORY — Past Simple
🏝️ The Race That Split an Island
🇫🇷 Saint-Martin
French side — bigger (53 km²)
🇳🇱 Sint Maarten
Dutch side (34 km²)

Long ago, pirates sailed the Caribbean Sea. They loved Saint Martin because it had salt, fresh water, and safe harbours. Christopher Columbus spotted the island on November 11, 1493 — Saint Martin's Day. He never landed, but he gave the island its name.


For many years, Spain, France, and the Netherlands all wanted the island. They fought many times. Spain built a fort and tried to keep the others away. But in 1648, Spain left. France and the Netherlands finally made a deal.


But how did they decide the border? They chose a very unusual method — a race! 🏃


A French runner and a Dutch runner both stood at the coast. They started running in opposite directions around the island. The border became the place where they met. The French runner ran faster — so France got the bigger part! Or… did he cheat? The legend says he drank wine before the race to give him courage. Nobody really knew the truth! 😄


On March 23, 1648, the two nations signed the Treaty of Concordia on Concordia Hill. They agreed: no fence, no passport, open border forever. And today — more than 370 years later — it is still true!

⚡ GRAMMAR — Past Simple
📘 How to use Past Simple
✅ Affirmative
Subject + past verb
The runner ran fast.  ·  France got the bigger part.
They signed the treaty.  ·  Columbus spotted the island.
Le coureur courut vite. · La France obtint la plus grande partie.
❌ Negative
Subject + didn't + base verb
Columbus didn't land on the island.
Spain didn't want to leave.
Colomb n'a pas débarqué. · L'Espagne ne voulait pas partir.
❓ Question
Did + subject + base verb?
Did France win the race? → Yes, it did. / No, it didn't.
Did Columbus land on the island? → No, he didn't.
Est-ce que la France a gagné ? · Colomb a-t-il débarqué ?
🔍 Wh- Question
Wh- word + did + subject + base verb?
Where did they sign the treaty?
When did Columbus spot the island?
Why did pirates love Saint Martin?
Où ont-ils signé le traité ? · Quand Colomb a-t-il aperçu l'île ?
