Chapter 2

Present Simple + Have Got

Two structures, one job: describing a person's permanent traits and what they've got.

Deux structures, un seul travail : décrire les traits permanents d'une personne et ce qu'elle possède.
The two structures side by side
Present Simple
Pour des faits, des habitudes, des caractéristiques
Subject + verb (+s for he/she/it)
He plays as a striker. He is very fast. He doesn't dive.
Have Got
Pour ce qu'une personne possède — physiquement ou comme qualité
Subject + have/has got + noun
He has got a powerful shot. They have got great chemistry.
Have got — full conjugation
SubjectPositiveNegativeQuestion
I / You / We / Theyhave got ('ve got)haven't gotHave you got…?
He / She / Ithas got ('s got)hasn't gotHas he got…?
Present simple: Mbappé plays for France. He doesn't waste chances.
Have got: He has got incredible acceleration. He hasn't got a weak foot — both feet are dangerous.
Question: "Has he got good vision too?" — "Yes, he has."
Common mistakes
⚠️ Watch out for these
He have got a strong shot.
✓ He has got a strong shot. "Has", pas "have", pour he/she/it.
He is got quick feet.
✓ He has got quick feet. / He is quick. Pas de "is" avec "got" — choisis une des deux structures.
He don't play for Barcelona anymore.
✓ He doesn't play for Barcelona anymore. "Doesn't", pas "don't", pour he/she/it.
