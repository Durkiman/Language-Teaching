📖 READING — NYC Food Culture
🍕 The Food Capital of the World

New York City is one of the most famous cities in the world. More than 8 million people live there, and they all need to eat! The city has more than 26,000 restaurants.


New Yorkers love their pizza. A classic New York slice is big, thin, and foldable — you always fold it in half before you eat it! The city has hundreds of pizza joints — small shops that only serve pizza by the slice.


The bagel is another New York icon. A bagel is a round bread with a hole in the middle. New Yorkers eat bagels for breakfast — usually with cream cheese and smoked salmon. The best bagels come from bakeries in Brooklyn.


On the streets, you can find hot dog vendors on almost every corner. A hot dog costs about $2. Food trucks roam the streets of Manhattan every day — the city never sleeps, and neither do its kitchens!

📋 RESTAURANT PHRASE LIST
🗣️ Everything you need to know — in a restaurant

From walking in to walking out. Learn these phrases — you'll use them for life!

🚪 Arriving
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Welcome! How many?"
(Bienvenue ! Vous êtes combien ?)
"Table for how many?""Just you today?"
🧒 You
"Just me, please." / "Two, please."
(Juste moi, s'il vous plaît. / Deux personnes.)
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Right this way!" / "Follow me, please."
(Par ici ! / Suivez-moi, s'il vous plaît.)
🧒 You
"Do you have a table by the window?"
(Vous avez une table près de la fenêtre ?)
💡 Optional — only ask if you want a specific spot!
📜 Looking at the menu
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Can I start you off with something to drink?"
(Je vous apporte quelque chose à boire pour commencer ?)
"What can I get you to drink?"
🧒 You
"Can I have a Coke, please?" / "Just water, thanks."
(Je peux avoir un Coca ? / Juste de l'eau, merci.)
🧒 You
"Can I have a few more minutes?"
(Je peux avoir encore quelques minutes ?)
💡 Say this if you're not ready to order yet!
🧒 You
"What do you recommend?"
(Qu'est-ce que vous recommandez ?)
🧒 You
"What's in the [dish name]?"
(Il y a quoi dans le [nom du plat] ?)
🍽️ Ordering
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Are you ready to order?" / "What can I get you?"
(Vous êtes prêt à commander ? / Qu'est-ce que je vous sers ?)
"What'll it be?""What are you having?"
🧒 You
"Can I get a [item], please?"
(Je peux avoir un(e) [plat], s'il vous plaît ?)
"I'll have the...""I'd like the...""Give me a..." (casual)
💡 "Can I get" is the most natural, everyday way to order in NYC!
🧑‍🍳 Server
"How would you like that cooked?"
(Vous le voulez cuit comment ?)
💡 For burgers/steaks: rare, medium-rare, medium, well-done
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Anything else?" / "Is that everything?"
(Autre chose ? / C'est tout ?)
"That all for you?"
🧒 You
"That's it, thank you." / "No, that's everything."
(C'est tout, merci. / Non, c'est tout.)
🥄 During the meal
🧑‍🍳 Server
"How is everything?" / "How's the food?"
(Tout se passe bien ? / Comment c'est ?)
🧒 You
"It's delicious, thank you!" / "Really good!"
(C'est délicieux, merci ! / Vraiment bon !)
🧒 You
"Excuse me, could I get some more water?"
(Excusez-moi, je pourrais avoir encore de l'eau ?)
"Can I get a refill?"
🧒 You
"Excuse me, I ordered the [X], not the [Y]."
(Excusez-moi, j'avais commandé le [X], pas le [Y].)
💡 Always be polite if there's a mistake — use "excuse me" first!
💵 Paying
🧒 You
"Can I get the check, please?"
(Je peux avoir l'addition, s'il vous plaît ?)
"Can we get the bill?" (British)"Check, please!"
💡 In the USA: always say "check" — "bill" sounds British!
🧑‍🍳 Server
"That comes to $12.50." / "Your total is $12.50."
(Ça fait 12,50 dollars. / Votre total est de 12,50 dollars.)
🧒 You
"Here you go. I've got a twenty."
(Voilà. J'ai un billet de 20.)
"Here's $20.""Keep the change!" (si tu veux laisser un pourboire)
🧑‍🍳 Server
"And here's your change — $7.50."
(Et voici votre monnaie — 7,50 dollars.)
💳 Both
"Can we split the bill?" / "Separate checks, please."
(On peut séparer l'addition ? / Des additions séparées, s'il vous plaît.)
💡 In the USA, "splitting the check" is very common with friends!
👋 Leaving
🧑‍🍳 Server
"Have a great day!" / "Come back soon!"
(Bonne journée ! / Revenez bientôt !)
🧒 You
"Thank you, it was delicious!" / "Great food, thanks!"
(Merci, c'était délicieux ! / Super repas, merci !)

🧠 GRAMMAR REVISION — Past Simple
💬 Explain it to me! — Teacher-led revision

Ask Micha each question out loud. Let him answer first, then tap Reveal.

1
How do you make an affirmative sentence in the Past Simple?
💬 "If I want to say something happened in the past, what do I do to the verb?"
Subject + past verb (regular: verb + -ed · irregular: learn the form!)
✅ I ordered a pizza.
✅ The waiter brought my food.
✅ We paid with a $10 bill.
J'ai commandé une pizza. · Le serveur a apporté ma nourriture.
⚠️ Same form for ALL subjects — no +s for he/she/it in the past!
He ordered (not orders) · She paid (not pays)
2
How do you make a negative in the Past Simple?
💬 "How do you say something did NOT happen?"
Subject + didn't + base verb (NOT the past form!)
✅ I didn't order the hot dog.
✅ He didn't pay with a card.
❌ She didn't ordered dessert. → didn't order
Je n'ai pas commandé le hot dog. · Il n'a pas payé par carte.
⚠️ The biggest trap: didn't + BASE verb — never past form!
didn't eat (not didn't ate) · didn't go (not didn't went)
3
How do you make a Yes/No question in the Past Simple?
💬 "What word do you put at the start?"
Did + subject + base verb? → Yes, … did. / No, … didn't.
✅ Did you order the pizza? → Yes, I did.
✅ Did the waiter bring ketchup? → No, he didn't.
❌ Did you ordered? → Did you order?
Est-ce que tu as commandé la pizza ? → Oui. / Non, il n'en a pas apporté.
⚠️ Did + BASE verb always — never the past form in the question!
4
Can you give me 5 irregular verbs — with their past form?
💬 Let Micha recall freely. Then reveal to check.
go → went
eat → ate
pay → paid
bring → brought
buy → bought
give → gave
come → came
find → found
have → had
make → made
leave → left
sell → sold
say → said
sit → sat
become → became
