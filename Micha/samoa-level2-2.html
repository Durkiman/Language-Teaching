Part 2 📖 Reading
⚡ Text A — Easier
Who Are Manu Samoa?
The Samoa national rugby team is called Manu Samoa. "Manu" means a person who is strong and respected — like a warrior.

Samoa played their first international rugby match in 1924 against Fiji. Since then, they became one of the most exciting teams in world rugby.

Before every match, the players performed a traditional war dance called the Siva Tau. It is similar to the New Zealand Haka. The players shout, stamp their feet, and show their strength to the other team.

Manu Samoa wore blue and white jerseys. Their biggest moment came in the 1991 Rugby World Cup, when they beat Wales and Argentina to reach the quarter-finals!
🏆 Text B — More Challenging
A Shocking World Cup Moment
In 1991, Western Samoa arrived at the Rugby World Cup as unknowns. Nobody knew much about this small Pacific island nation of just 160,000 people. But that was about to change.

In their first match, Samoa ran onto the pitch against Wales — one of rugby's great traditional powers. The Welsh team got a shock: Samoa won 16–13 in Cardiff. The Welsh fans couldn't believe it. One supporter even said, "Thank goodness we weren't playing all of Samoa!"

Samoa also beat Argentina 35–12 and reached the quarter-finals. They eventually lost to Scotland, but the world took notice. Rugby fans found a new team to admire — and Manu Samoa became legends.
