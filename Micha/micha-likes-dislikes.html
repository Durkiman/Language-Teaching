Likes & Dislikes 🎒

Packing for the trip — food, drinks & hobbies

Card: A1-04 — Likes & dislikes
Goal: Can say what he/she likes, loves and hates.
Grammar focus: verb + -ing with like/love/hate
Evidence task: Six true like/dislike sentences across two topics, at least three with verb + -ing.

Get ready 🧳

Imagine your squad is going on a big adventure trip — new city, new food, free time between games. Before we plan it, let's warm up:

  • Name 3 foods you already know in English.
  • Name 2 hobbies or free-time activities you already know.
  • Quick chat: "Do you like pizza?" — "Yes, I love it!" / "No, I hate it."

like / love / hate + verb-ing

🇫🇷 aimer bien / adorer / détester + verbe en -ing

Subject + like / love / hate + verb-ing
  • I like eating pizza. 🇫🇷 J'aime bien manger de la pizza.
  • He loves playing football. 🇫🇷 Il adore jouer au football.
  • They hate losing. 🇫🇷 Ils détestent perdre.
  • She doesn't like waiting. 🇫🇷 Elle n'aime pas attendre.

💡 How to build the -ing form

  • eat → eating · play → playing
  • swim → swimming (double letter) · dance → dancing (drop the e)
⚠ Common mistake: Don't add "to" — say "I like eating", not "I like to eating".

⚽ Hobbies

📖 Reading: Before the Match

Meet Leo, a young footballer getting ready for a tournament abroad. He loves eating pasta the night before a game — it gives him energy. He likes drinking water all day, not juice. In his free time, he loves listening to music to relax, and he likes playing video games with his teammates. But he hates waiting at the airport before a trip — he says it's the worst part of travelling!
Easy
1. I ___ (like) eat pizza before a match.
🇫🇷 hint 💡 grammar
Aimer manger → verbe en -ing.
Remember: like + verb-ing, no "to".
Easy
2. She loves ___ (dance) at parties.
🇫🇷 hint 💡 grammar
danser → dancing (on enlève le "e").
Verbs ending in silent -e drop the e before -ing.
Medium
3. Choose the correct sentence:
💡 grammar
No "to" after hate, and "they" doesn't take -s.
Medium
4. We love ___ (travel) to new cities.
🇫🇷 hint
voyager → travelling (double l in British English).
Hard
5. Find and fix the mistake: "I like to swimming in the sea."
💡 grammar
Remove "to" — like is directly followed by verb-ing.
Hard
6. Choose the correct form: "He doesn't like ___ (wait) at the airport."
