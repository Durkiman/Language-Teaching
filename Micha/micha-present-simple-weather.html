What is a heat dome?

A heat dome is a bubble of hot air trapped over Europe. It acts like a lid on a pot — it keeps hot air in and does not let it escape.

Un dôme de chaleur est une bulle d'air chaud piégée au-dessus de l'Europe. Il agit comme un couvercle sur une casserole.

The hot air comes from the Sahara Desert in Africa. It travels north and gets trapped by the high pressure system above Europe.

L'air chaud vient du Sahara en Afrique. Il voyage vers le nord et reste coincé par un système de haute pression au-dessus de l'Europe.

🌊 What is El Niño?

El Niño means "The Little Boy" in Spanish. It is a natural climate pattern that starts in the Pacific Ocean — and it affects the weather all over the world, including Europe.

El Niño signifie "Le Petit Garçon" en espagnol. C'est un phénomène climatique naturel qui commence dans l'océan Pacifique.

Normally:

Strong trade winds blow warm water west across the Pacific, towards Asia. The eastern Pacific (near South America) stays cool.

Les vents alizés soufflent l'eau chaude vers l'ouest. Le Pacifique est frais près de l'Amérique du Sud.

During El Niño:

The trade winds weaken. The warm water moves east instead. The whole Pacific gets warmer — and this pushes hot air and warm weather all the way to Europe.

Les vents alizés s'affaiblissent. L'eau chaude se déplace vers l'est. Tout le Pacifique se réchauffe — et cela pousse l'air chaud jusqu'en Europe.

🔗 El Niño + Belgium's heatwave:

El Niño warms the whole planet slightly. Combined with the heat dome over Europe, it makes heatwaves like this one more extreme and more likely.

El Niño réchauffe légèrement toute la planète. Combiné avec le dôme de chaleur, il rend les canicules plus extrêmes.

📐 Present Simple — Weather Facts

Le présent simple pour parler de la météo

When do we use it? For things that are always true or generally true — facts about weather, seasons, and habits.

Quand on l'utilise ? Pour des vérités générales — des faits sur la météo, les saisons, les habitudes.

subject + verb (base form)  |  He/She/It + verb + -s
SubjectFormExample
I / You / We / TheyverbIt rains in Belgium a lot.
He / She / Itverb + sThe sun shines every day in July.

Il pleut beaucoup en Belgique. · Le soleil brille tous les jours en juillet.

Special weather verbs

Some weather verbs use "It" as the subject — there is no real subject, "it" is just a placeholder.

Certains verbes météo utilisent "It" comme sujet — c'est un sujet vide, comme "il" dans "il pleut".

ExpressionMeaningFrench
It rainsRain is fallingIl pleut
It snowsSnow is fallingIl neige
It is hotThe temperature is highIl fait chaud
It is sunnyThe sun is outIl fait soleil
It is windyThere is windIl y a du vent
⚠️ Common mistakes:

❌ "It hot." → ✅ "It is hot." (never forget "is"!)

❌ "The weather are nice." → ✅ "The weather is nice." (weather = singular)

❌ "It rain." → ✅ "It rains." (add -s with "it"!)

N'oublie jamais "is" · "weather" est singulier · Ajoute -s avec "it"

📝 Reading — The Orange Alert

Lecture — L'alerte orange

Belgium is in a heatwave. The sun shines all day and the temperature reaches 35°C. It is very humid and there is almost no wind. People drink a lot of water to stay safe. In France, it is even hotter — up to 44°C in some places. Schools close early and people stay inside. Scientists say this kind of heat is going to happen more often in the future.

La Belgique est en canicule. Le soleil brille toute la journée et la température atteint 35°C...

Hard

6. Present Simple or Going to? Choose and explain why.

"It _____ (be) very hot in Belgium every summer." vs "It _____ (be) 40°C tomorrow."

First blank = is (general fact) · Second blank = is going to be (prediction)
Both = is going to be (both are predictions)
Both = is (both are facts)
💡 Grammar tip
Present Simple = general truth (every summer = always true). Going to = specific future prediction (tomorrow = a plan/forecast).
🇫🇷 French hint
Fait général → présent simple. Prédiction spécifique → "going to".
