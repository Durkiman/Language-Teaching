Reading · 10 min

The story of Linkin Park.

Read the text. Notice the coloured verbs.

Irregular verb

Time marker

In 1996, a group of friends in California began a band called Xero. They practised in a garage and wrote their own songs, mixing rock, rap, and electronic sounds. At first, they didn't find success right away. Several singers came and went before they met Chester Bennington, a singer from Arizona with a powerful voice.

In 2000, the band, now called Linkin Park, released their first album, Hybrid Theory. It became one of the best-selling debut albums in history. Millions of young people connected with the emotional lyrics and the mix of heavy guitars and electronic beats. The band toured the world and grew into one of the biggest rock bands of the decade.

Over the next years, Linkin Park continued to change their sound. They experimented with electronic music, hip-hop, and pop influences. Some fans didn't like every new direction, but the band always chose to follow their own creative instincts rather than repeat the same formula.

In July 2017, Chester Bennington died. It was a huge shock for fans around the world. For a long time, the remaining members didn't perform together, and many people didn't know if the band would continue at all.

In 2024, Linkin Park returned with a new singer, Emily Armstrong. It was a difficult and emotional decision for the band, but they felt ready to create music again. Fans reacted with a mix of excitement and complicated emotions — the band's story had changed forever, but the music meant as much as ever.
