What I did last week.

Read the text. Notice the coloured verbs — they're all in the past simple.

Last week I had one of the most intense weeks of my year. On Monday, I got a call from a client — he told me there was a big problem with the sound system at a venue. I didn't know exactly what the problem was, so I took all my equipment and went there straight away.

At first, nothing worked. I checked everything — the cables, the mixers, the monitors. I spent almost two hours on it. The client didn't say much, but I could feel the pressure.

Then, I found the issue. One small cable caused the whole problem. I fixed it in five minutes. After that, the sound came back perfectly. The client smiled — he looked so relieved.

In the end, it was a great day. I didn't sleep much that night, but I felt really good about the work. Sometimes the small things make the biggest difference.

Rule 01 — Affirmative: Regular Verbs

Regular verbs follow a simple rule: add -ed to the base form. Always. No exceptions.

Formula
Subject + verb + -ed
I played a set at the festival.
J'ai joué un set au festival.
He worked all night.
Il a travaillé toute la nuit.
We arrived late.
Nous sommes arrivés tard.
The crowd loved the music.
La foule a adoré la musique.
⚠️ Common mistake: "I play a set yesterday" → The verb stays in present simple. Always change to past: "I played".
Spelling rules for -ed
Most verbs → add -ed   work → worked, play → played
Ends in -e → add -d   arrive → arrived, love → loved
Consonant + y → -ied   try → tried, carry → carried
Short vowel + consonant → double + -ed   stop → stopped, drop → dropped
Rule 02 — Affirmative: Irregular Verbs

Irregular verbs don't follow the -ed rule. Each has its own past form — the most common ones come naturally with practice.

Formula
Subject + irregular past form
I went to a festival last weekend.
Je suis allé à un festival le week-end dernier.
He told me about the show.
Il m'a parlé du spectacle.
We heard an incredible set.
Nous avons entendu un set incroyable.

Rule 03 — Negative

For all verbs — regular and irregular — use didn't + base form. The main verb goes back to base form. Never add -ed after didn't.

Formula
Subject + didn't + verb (base form)
I didn't go to the main stage.
Je ne suis pas allé à la scène principale.
He didn't play until midnight.
Il n'a pas joué avant minuit.
We didn't sleep at all.
Nous n'avons pas du tout dormi.
It didn't sound right at first.
Ça ne sonnait pas bien au début.
⚠️ Common mistake: "I didn't went" or "I didn't played" → After didn't, always use base form: "I didn't go", "I didn't play".
Rule 04 — Time Markers

These words signal you're talking about the past, and help your story flow — use them to connect events.

first
d'abord
"First, I set up the sound system."
then
ensuite / puis
"Then we went to the stage."
after that
après ça
"After that, everything changed."
suddenly
soudain / tout à coup
"Suddenly, the music stopped."
in the end
finalement / au final
"In the end, it was amazing."
last week
la semaine dernière
"Last week I worked at a festival."
yesterday

"Yesterday I didn't sleep much."
at first
au début
"At first I didn't understand the problem."
