Reading · 10 min

Dour Festival 2026.

English speakers use short verb + particle combinations like these constantly — they sound much more natural than the "full" verb. Notice the highlighted ones as you read.

Dour Festival kicked off on the 15th of July 2026 and wrapped up five days later, on the 19th. The site — Plaine de la Machine à Feu, in the small town of Dour, Belgium — filled up with around 150,000 people across the week, spread over 9 different stages. The festival first started up back in 1988 with just five bands. It has grown enormously since then.

The 2026 lineup brought together a huge range of styles — from drum and bass acts like Pendulum, to French rap with Damso and Orelsan, to electronic names like Amelie Lens and Sammy Virji. Crews had to set up and plug in equipment for dozens of acts across the different stages, often with only a short window to turn around between sets.

With that many stages running at once, things inevitably broke down here and there — a cable, a monitor, a mixer overheating in the summer sun. Technicians had to sort out problems fast, sometimes with thousands of people waiting. When a set cut out unexpectedly, the crew backstage had only seconds to figure out what had gone wrong before the crowd noticed.

By the final night, the festival began to wind down. Crews across the site started to pack up stages that had been running non-stop for days. Many festival-goers stayed up until sunrise for the last sets, before slowly heading off home — already looking forward to next year.

Grammar Focus — Separable vs Inseparable

Some of today's phrasal verbs can split around their object; others can't.

Separable — the object can go between verb and particle, and must if it's a pronoun: "They set up the stage." → "They set it up." (never "set up it")
Inseparable — the object always comes after the particle: "The crew ran into a problem." → "They ran into it." (never "ran it into")
Part A — Gap Fill
01
The festival ______ ______ on the 15th of July and ran for five days.
Le festival a ______ ______ le 15 juillet et a duré cinq jours.
02
The site ______ ______ with around 150,000 people across the week.
Le site s'est ______ ______ avec environ 150 000 personnes sur la semaine.
03
With so many stages running, equipment sometimes ______ ______ during a set.
Avec autant de scènes en activité, le matériel ______ parfois ______ pendant un set.
04
Technicians had to ______ ______ problems fast, with thousands of people waiting.
Les techniciens devaient ______ ______ les problèmes rapidement, avec des milliers de personnes qui attendaient.
05
When a set ______ ______ unexpectedly, the crew had seconds to react.
Quand un set s'est ______ ______ de façon inattendue, l'équipe n'avait que quelques secondes pour réagir.
06
By the final night, the festival began to ______ ______, and crews started to ______ ______ the stages.
Vers la dernière nuit, le festival a commencé à ______ ______, et les équipes ont commencé à ______ ______ les scènes.
