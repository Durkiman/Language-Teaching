To Be

Am, Is, Are — Identity, Description & Existence

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

To be is the most important verb in English — it links a subject to who or what they are. It has no auxiliary "do": am/is/are itself moves to make negatives and questions.

identity
I am a teacher. She is my sister.
description
He is tall. The film is boring.
state / feeling
They are happy. I am tired.
location
We are at home. It is on the table.
Careful: no "do" with "to be". Are you tired? not Do you are tired? I am not tired. not I don't am tired.

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: what does this use of "be" express?
  1. "Here you are." a) idiomatic — handing something over  b) literal location
  2. "If I were you, I'd wait." a) hypothetical (subjunctive)  b) past state
  3. "Be that as it may…" a) formal concession  b) location
  4. "So be it." a) formal acceptance  b) command
  5. "It is what it is." a) resigned acceptance  b) description
  6. "Whatever the case may be…" a) formal hedging  b) identity
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.

  3. What is important are the results.
  4. Neither the manager nor the staff was informed.
  5. The reason is because the data was wrong.
