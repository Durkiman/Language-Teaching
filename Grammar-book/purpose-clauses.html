Purpose Clauses

To, In Order To, So That — Explaining Why

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Purpose clauses answer the question "why?" — they explain the goal behind an action. Use to + verb when the subject is the same; use so that when a different person is involved.

to + verb
I called to ask about the job.
in order to / so as to (formal)
She arrived early in order to prepare.
negative purpose
He left quietly so as not to wake anyone.
so that + subject + can/could
I'll email you the file so that you can check it.
Careful: "for" + noun, not verb. I went to the shop to buy bread. not for buy bread. Use for only before a noun: I went to the shop for bread.

  4. He studies (to / for) pass the exam.
  5. They came (to / for) help us.

b. to see the doctor.
3. He went to the clinic
c. to borrow books.
4. We saved money
d. to buy a car.

LEVEL A2 · Elementary
Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

Ana got up early to catch the first train. She left a note for her flatmate so that he wouldn't worry. At the station, she bought a coffee to stay awake, and put on headphones so that she could work without noise. She had planned everything carefully in order to arrive on time for her interview.

  1. Ana got up early to catch the first train. ___
  2. She left a note for her flatmate to worry him. ___
  3. She bought coffee to stay awake. ___
  4. She used headphones so that she could work without noise. ___
  5. Her plan was for arriving late on purpose. ___

  1. I woke up early. I wanted to catch the bus.
  2. She whispered. She didn't want to wake the baby.
  3. We booked tickets in advance. We wanted our kids to get good seats.
  4. He turned off his phone. He wanted to concentrate.

Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

  1. Flexible hours were introduced to improve retention. ___
