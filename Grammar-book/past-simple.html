The Past Simple

Regular & Irregular Verbs

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

Regular verbs add -ed; irregular verbs change form entirely and must be memorised. The past simple describes a completed action at a specific past time.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + verb-ed / irregular past I walked home. / She went home.
Negative subject + didn't + base verb He didn't call me.
Question Did + subject + base verb ? Did you see him?
Spelling note (regular verbs): most verbs add -ed (walk → walked); verbs ending in -e add only -d (live → lived); consonant + -y → -ied (study → studied); short stressed CVC verbs double the consonant (stop → stopped).

When do we use it?

✅ Completed action at a specific time
I visited Rome in 2019.
🔗 A sequence of past actions
She woke up, showered, and left.
🔁 A past habit (finished now)
We played tennis every Sunday.
⏳ A finished duration in the past
I lived in Cairo for six years.
📜 A historical fact
The war ended in 1945.
🎯 The main event in a story
Suddenly, the lights went out.
⚡ Regular or irregular?
Regular

Follows the -ed pattern predictably.

work → worked, watch → watched

Irregular

Changes form entirely — no rule, must be learned.

go → went, buy → bought, be → was/were

Pronunciation of -ed: /t/ after voiceless sounds (watched), /d/ after voiced sounds (played), /ɪd/ after t/d (wanted, needed).
