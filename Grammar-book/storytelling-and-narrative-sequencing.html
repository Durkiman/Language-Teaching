Storytelling & Narrative Sequencing

Linking Past Events

📘 1. Understanding the Skill

Past simple
Main events, in order: "She opened the door."
Past continuous
Background/scene-setting: "It was raining."
Past perfect
An earlier event, before the main story time: "She had already left."

Sequencing words connect events clearly: One day, First, Then, Next, After that, Later, Meanwhile, While, Eventually, In the end, Finally.

Tip: use the past perfect only for an event that happened before the main timeline of your story — not just for anything in the past.

Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

One day, Maria decided to go for a walk. First, she put on her shoes. Then, she left the house and walked to the park. While she was walking, she saw an old friend. They talked for a while, and after that, they had coffee together. Eventually, Maria went home, feeling happy.

  1. Maria decided to go for a walk.
  2. She saw her friend before leaving the house.
  3. She was walking when she saw her friend.
  4. They had coffee before talking.
  5. Maria felt happy at the end.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

By the time Elena reached the station, the train had already left, so she decided to wait for the next one. While she was waiting on the platform, she noticed an elderly man who seemed lost; he had been searching for his ticket for several minutes and couldn't find it. She offered to help, and together they eventually found it in his coat pocket, where it had fallen through a hole in the lining. Grateful, the man thanked her before boarding his train, and Elena, still smiling, boarded hers ten minutes later.

  1. Elena's train had already left when she arrived.
  2. She ignored the elderly man and walked away.
  3. The man had been searching for his ticket for a while before Elena noticed.
  4. The ticket was found in the man's bag.
  5. Elena boarded her train after the man boarded his.
