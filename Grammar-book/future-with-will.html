Future with "Will"

Predictions, Promises & Decisions

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

"Will" is a modal verb: it's the same for every subject and is always followed by the base verb, with no "to" and no -s.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + will + base verb I will help you. (I'll help)
Negative subject + will not/won't + base verb She won't be late.
Question Will + subject + base verb ? Will you help me?

When do we use it?

🔮 Predictions (opinion, no evidence)
I think it will rain later.
⚡ Decisions made right now
The phone's ringing — I'll get it.
🤝 Promises, offers & threats
I'll always be there for you.
🙏 Polite requests
Will you pass the salt, please?
Careful with time clauses: after when, if, after, before, as soon as, until, unless, once, use the present simple, not "will" — even though the meaning is future. I will call you when I arrive. (not "when I will arrive")

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: what does "will" express here?
  1. "Boys will be boys." a) a characteristic tendency  b) a promise
  2. "He said he would call later." — "would" here is: a) future-in-the-past  b) a conditional
  3. "The results will be announced at noon, as scheduled." a) formal certainty/schedule  b) spontaneous decision
  4. "This medication will cause drowsiness in some patients." a) a typical tendency  b) a one-time promise
  5. "Ice will melt at room temperature." a) a scientific/general truth  b) an offer
  6. "I will have this finished by Friday, I assure you." a) firm commitment  b) polite request
