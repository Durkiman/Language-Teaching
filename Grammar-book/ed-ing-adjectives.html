-ed / -ing Adjectives

Feeling It vs Causing It

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Adjectives made from verbs come in two forms: -ed describes how someone feels; -ing describes what causes that feeling in someone or something else.

-ed = the feeling
I am bored. She is interested.
-ing = the cause
The film is boring. The topic is interesting.
material — "made of"
The table is made of wood.
purpose — "used for + -ing"
A whisk is used for beating eggs.
Careful: "I'm boring" ≠ "I'm bored". I'm boring means you cause boredom in others (an insult!). I'm bored means you feel bored. Always check who feels it and who/what causes it.
