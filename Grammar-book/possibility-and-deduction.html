Possibility & Deduction

Might, May, Could, Must, Can't — Guessing Right

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

These modals let you guess how likely something is — from "maybe" to "definitely" — about the present or the past. Past forms add have + past participle.

possible, not sure (now)
She might/may/could be at home.
almost certain (now)
She must be at home (the lights are on). / She can't be at work (it's Sunday).
possible, not sure (past)
She might have/could have left already.
almost certain (past)
She must have left. / She can't have left — her coat's still here.
Careful: "should have" ≠ "must have". He must have missed the bus = a deduction (I'm guessing why). He should have called = criticism/regret (he didn't do it, and I think he was wrong not to).

Deduction vs criticism — don't mix them up

FormMeaning
He must have forgotten.deduction — I'm guessing that's what happened

  1. The writer is 100% sure Tom is at work. ___
  2. The office is closed today. ___
  3. Tom might have gone to his parents'. ___
  4. His phone is on. ___
  5. The writer thinks he's probably somewhere without signal. ___
