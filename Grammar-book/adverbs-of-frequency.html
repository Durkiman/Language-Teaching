Adverbs of Frequency

Position & Usage

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens. Their position in the sentence depends on the type of verb.

Verb type Position Example
Main verb (not "be") before the verb She always walks to work.
Verb "to be" after the verb He is never late.
Auxiliary + main verb between auxiliary and main verb I have never seen that.
Longer expressions (every day, once a week, from time to time) usually go at the end of the sentence, not before the verb: "I go swimming twice a week" (not "I twice a week go swimming").

The frequency scale

💯 100% — always
She always drinks tea in the morning.
🔵 ~90% — usually/normally
I usually take the bus.
🟠 ~70% — often/frequently
They often eat out on Fridays.
🟡 ~50% — sometimes
He sometimes forgets his keys.
🟤 ~20% — rarely/seldom
We rarely go to the cinema.
⚫ 0% — never
She never eats meat.
Emphasis at the front: in formal or literary style, negative adverbs can start the sentence with subject-auxiliary inversion — "Never have I seen such chaos," "Rarely does he complain."

Position rules

TypePosition
Single-word adverbsbefore main verb / after "be" / mid-auxiliary
Phrases (every day, twice a week)end of sentence (sometimes start, for emphasis)
