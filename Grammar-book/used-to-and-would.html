Used to & Would

Talking About Past Habits

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Used to and would both describe repeated past actions or habits that no longer happen. Used to can also describe a past state; would cannot.

Form Structure Example
Used to (affirmative) subject + used to + base verb I used to play football.
Used to (negative/question) didn't use to / Did...use to She didn't use to like coffee.
Would subject + would + base verb We would visit our grandparents every summer.
Key restriction: "would" only works for repeated actions, never for states/situations. "I used to live in Rome" is correct; "I would live in Rome" is not.

Used to vs. would

🔁 Repeated action (both work)
I used to/would go jogging every morning.
🏠 State/situation (only used to)
I used to have long hair. (not "would have")
❤️ Feelings/beliefs (only used to)
She used to hate spiders.
📖 Storytelling scene (would common)
Every winter, he would light the fire at dusk.
⚡ Used to vs. be used to (careful!)
used to + verb

a past habit that has ended: I used to smoke.

be/get used to + -ing

being accustomed to something (any tense): I'm used to working late now.

Spelling reminder: "used to" (one d) is for past habits; "use to" appears only after did/didn't — "Did you use to live here?"
