Past Perfect

The Past Before the Past

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Past perfect (had + past participle) shows that one past action happened before another past action. It puts events in the right order when you're already talking about the past.

earlier action
She had left before I arrived.
cause of a past result
He was tired because he hadn't slept.
reported speech (backshift)
"I've finished." → She said she had finished.
regret (3rd conditional)
If I had known, I would have helped.
Careful: past perfect ≠ present perfect. Have/has connects to now. Had connects to an earlier point in the past — you need two past events for "had" to make sense.

LEVEL C1 · Advanced
Exercise 13 — Rewrite using inversion for emphasis (Scarcely had… / No sooner had…).

  2. We had just left when the storm started.
  3. He had hardly finished speaking when the crowd erupted.

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: what does the past perfect add here?
  1. "Had she only known…" a) formal, regretful hypothetical  b) simple past narration
  2. "It later emerged that he had lied." a) revealing something hidden until now  b) ongoing action
  3. "Not until the results came in had anyone suspected fraud." a) formal inversion for emphasis  b) casual statement
  4. "She wished she had said something." a) regret about the past  b) present possibility
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.

  2. Had she of known, she would have acted differently.
  3. It transpired that they had knew all along.
  4. Scarcely he had arrived when the trouble began.
  5. She regretted that she hadn't said anything sooner, isn't it.
