Linking Words

Connecting Ideas Clearly

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Linking words connect ideas within and between sentences. Each one belongs to a function (addition, contrast, cause, result, purpose, condition, sequence) — and each function has both everyday and formal options.

Addition
and, also, as well as, furthermore.
Contrast
but, although, however, despite, whereas.
Cause & Result
because, so, as a result, therefore.
Condition
if, unless, provided that, in case.
Careful: clause words vs noun words. Although / because + subject + verb (a clause). Despite / because of + noun or -ing (no verb after). "Although it was raining" ✓ but "Despite it was raining" ✗ — say "Despite the rain" or "Despite it raining."
Careful: "despite of" doesn't exist. It's just despite, or in spite of — never "despite of."
⚡ "So" (result) vs "so that" (purpose)
so (result)

It was cold, so I closed the window.

so that (purpose)

I closed the window so that it wouldn't get cold.

  1. You add the pasta before the water boils.
  2. You wait ten minutes after adding the pasta.
  3. The sauce is the last step.

LEVEL B1 · Intermediate (although / despite / because of)
Exercise 7 — Find and correct the mistake in each sentence.
  1. Despite it was raining, we went out.
  2. We stayed home because of it was cold.
  3. Although the traffic, we arrived on time.
  4. In spite of of the rain, the match continued.
  5. I closed the window so it wouldn't get cold, that's the reason.
Exercise 8 — Choose "although" or "despite."
  1. the heavy traffic, we arrived early.
  2. it was expensive, she bought it.
  3. his injury, he finished the race.
  4. we were tired, we kept working.
Exercise 9 — Multiple choice: so or so that?
  1. It was late, ___ we left.
  2. I saved money ___ I could travel.
  3. She whispered ___ nobody would hear.
LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate (whereas / nevertheless / provided that)
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

The plan will go ahead, provided that the budget is approved. The team will proceed unless new problems come up. Sales rose in the north, whereas they fell slightly in the south. Nevertheless, the company remains confident about the full year.

  1. The plan needs budget approval to go ahead.
  2. New problems would stop the team from proceeding.
  3. Sales fell in both regions.
  4. The company is confident despite the mixed results.
Exercise 11 — Find and correct the (subtler) mistake in each sentence.
  1. We'll proceed unless if the budget is cut.
  2. Provided the weather is good, we'll go, whereas otherwise we won't.
  3. In case it rains, we will bring an umbrella just in case.
  4. Sales rose, nevertheless costs rose too, however.
Exercise 12 — Guided writing.

Compare two cities, jobs, or habits in 6–8 sentences. Use at least six different linking words from at least three different functions (addition, contrast, cause/result, condition).

Exercise 16 — Open writing.
