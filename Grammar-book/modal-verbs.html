Modal Verbs

Can, Must, Have to, Should

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Modal verbs are followed by the base verb (no "to", no -s) and never change form.

can / could
Ability, permission, polite requests.
must
Strong obligation (the speaker's own view), deduction/certainty.
have to
Obligation from an outside rule or situation.
should
Advice, recommendation, mild obligation, expectation.
Careful: mustn't ≠ don't have to. Mustn't = it's forbidden. Don't have to = it's not necessary, but it's allowed.

  1. Students must wear a uniform.
  2. Students have to bring their own lunch.
  3. Students should arrive by 8 a.m.
  4. Students can use phones during class.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Employees must complete mandatory safety training within their first month, according to company policy, although those who have already completed equivalent training elsewhere don't have to repeat it. Managers should encourage staff to ask questions during the sessions, since misunderstanding safety procedures could have serious consequences. Staff mustn't operate heavy machinery without certification, regardless of prior experience. It's also recommended, though not required, that employees review the safety manual annually.

  1. All employees must complete safety training within their first month.
  2. Employees who completed equivalent training elsewhere must still repeat it.
  3. Staff can operate heavy machinery without certification if experienced.
  4. Reviewing the safety manual annually is mandatory.
  5. Managers are encouraged to invite questions during training.
