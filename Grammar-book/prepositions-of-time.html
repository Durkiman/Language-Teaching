Prepositions of Time

Talking About When

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

PrepositionUseExample
inmonths, years, seasons, parts of dayI was born in 1998.
ondays, datesThe meeting is on Monday.
atclock times, night, weekends (UK)We eat lunch at 1 p.m.
for / since
for + duration; since + starting point.
during / while
during + noun; while + clause.
by / until
by = no later than; until = up to that point.
before / after
Relative sequence in time.
Careful: "in time" = early enough to do something; "on time" = punctual, exactly on schedule.

  2. The concert is on a Saturday.
  3. Doors open after the concert starts.
  4. The show should end by 10 p.m.
  5. This is a winter concert.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Although the project began in early 2022, significant delays meant that the first phase wasn't completed until late 2023. During the intervening period, the team worked continuously, often late into the night, to meet interim deadlines. By the time the second phase starts, in the spring, the team hopes to have resolved most of the outstanding issues. Since the project's inception, the budget has been revised three times.

  1. The project began in early 2022.
  2. The first phase was completed on schedule.
  3. The team worked steadily throughout the delay.
  4. The second phase starts in the autumn.
  5. The budget has never been changed.
