Comparing People, Places & Things

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

Adjective typeComparativeSuperlative
Short (1 syllable)adj + -er (tall → taller)the + adj + -est (the tallest)
Long (2+ syllables)more + adj (more beautiful)the most + adj (the most beautiful)
Spelling note: single vowel + consonant → double the consonant (big → bigger); ends in -e → just add -r/-st (nice → nicer); consonant + -y → -ier/-iest (happy → happier).

Equality & degree

⚖️ as...as
She is as tall as her brother.
📏 Degree modifiers
much, a lot, far, slightly, a bit + comparative

Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

The Nile is the longest river in Africa. It is longer than the Amazon River, but the Amazon carries more water. Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world — it is much taller than any mountain in Europe. The Pacific Ocean is the biggest ocean, bigger than the Atlantic and the Indian Ocean combined.

  1. The Nile is shorter than the Amazon.
  2. The Amazon carries more water than the Nile.
  3. Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world.
  4. The Atlantic Ocean is bigger than the Pacific.
  5. European mountains are taller than Everest.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Although Tokyo is often considered one of the most expensive cities in the world, it is actually less costly than several European capitals when it comes to everyday groceries. Public transport, however, is far more efficient than in most Western cities, making daily commutes considerably less stressful. Housing remains the biggest financial burden for most residents, with prices in central districts rising faster than incomes. Despite this, surveys consistently rank Tokyo as one of the safest and most livable cities globally.

  1. Tokyo's groceries are more expensive than several European capitals.
  2. Public transport in Tokyo is more efficient than in most Western cities.
  3. Housing costs are rising slower than incomes.
  4. Tokyo is generally considered unsafe.
  5. Tokyo ranks among the most livable cities.
