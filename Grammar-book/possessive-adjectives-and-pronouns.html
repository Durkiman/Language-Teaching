Possessive Adjectives & Pronouns

Showing Ownership

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Possessive adjectives come before a noun (my book). Possessive pronouns replace a noun entirely and stand alone (This book is mine).

SubjectPossessive adjectivePossessive pronoun
Imymine
youyouryours
he / she / ithis / her / itshis / hers / —
weourours
theytheirtheirs
Common mix-ups: its (possessive, no apostrophe) vs it's (= it is/has); whose (possessive) vs who's (= who is/has); your (possessive) vs you're (= you are). None of the possessive pronouns (mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs) ever take an apostrophe.

Contraction vs possessive at a glance

Possessive (no apostrophe)Contraction (apostrophe = missing letters)
its (belonging to it)it's (it is / it has)
whose (belonging to whom)who's (who is / who has)

LEVEL A2 · Elementary
Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

Sam and Lucy share a flat. Sam's room is small, but hers is bigger. His bed is next to the window; her desk is near the door. The kitchen is theirs to share, and the old sofa in the living room is ours, according to the landlord. Whose turn is it to clean? It's mine this week.

  1. Sam's room is bigger than Lucy's.
  2. Sam's bed is next to the window.
  3. The kitchen belongs only to Sam.
  4. The speaker has to clean this week.
  5. The sofa belongs to the landlord alone.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Although the two companies merged last year, each division retained its own management structure; the branding, however, became entirely theirs to redesign collectively. Employees whose contracts predated the merger kept their original benefits, while newer staff had to accept whichever terms were offered. It's often unclear, even to long-time employees, whose decisions ultimately shape company policy.

  1. Each division kept its own management structure.
  2. The branding remained separate for each company.
  3. All employees, regardless of hiring date, kept the same benefits.
  4. It is always completely clear whose decisions shape policy.
  5. The companies merged last year.
