The "Going to" Future

vs. Will

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

"Going to" is built with am/is/are + going to + base verb. It talks about the future from a present starting point — a plan already made, or a prediction based on what we can see now.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + am/is/are + going to + verb She is going to travel. / I am going to travel.
Negative subject + am/is/are + not + going to + verb She isn't going to travel.
Question Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb ? Is she going to travel?

When do we use it?

📝 Plans decided before now
I'm going to visit my grandmother next week.
👀 Predictions from present evidence
Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.
⚡ "Going to" vs. "will"
Going to

A plan already decided, or a prediction backed by evidence you can see now.

We've booked the flights — we're going to visit Rome.

Will

A decision made at the moment of speaking, a promise/offer, or a prediction based on opinion.

The phone's ringing — I'll answer it. / I think Rome will be lovely in spring.

Quick test: if you can point to a booking, a ticket, a memo, or dark clouds as your reason, use going to. If you're deciding right now, promising, or just guessing, use will.
