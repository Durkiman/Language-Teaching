The Present Continuous

vs. Present Simple

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

The present continuous is built with am/is/are + verb-ing. It describes an action happening now or around now.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + am/is/are + verb-ing She is cooking dinner.
Negative subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing I am not watching TV.
Question Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing ? Are you listening?
Spelling note: drop a silent -e before -ing (write → writing); double the final consonant after a stressed short vowel (run → running); -ie → -ying (lie → lying).

When do we use it?

🎬 Happening right now
Look — it's raining!
📅 Temporary situation around now
I'm reading a great book this month.
🗓️ Fixed future arrangement
We're meeting them on Friday.
📈 A changing/developing trend
The climate is getting warmer.
😤 Annoying repeated habits (+ always)
He's always losing his keys.
🎯 Setting the scene for a story
The sun is setting behind the hills.
⚡ Continuous or simple?
Present Continuous

Temporary, in progress, around now.

I am working from home this week.

Present Simple

Permanent, habitual, a fact.

I work from home every Friday.

Stative verbs: verbs of thought, emotion, and the senses (know, want, believe, love, seem, own) are not normally used in the continuous — say "I want this" not "I am wanting this."
