Asking Better Questions

Open vs. Closed Questions & CCQs

📘 1. Understanding the Skill

🔒 Closed questions
Get a yes/no or one-word answer: "Do you like tea?"
🔓 Open questions
Invite explanation: "What do you enjoy about your job?"

A good conversation mixes both. Too many closed questions can feel like an interrogation; open questions keep the conversation flowing.

🔎 What is a CCQ?

A Concept Checking Question is a specific, simple question used to confirm someone has really understood an idea — instead of the almost-useless "Do you understand?" (which nearly always gets an automatic "yes"). Good CCQs use simpler language than the idea being checked, and test it from different angles. Teaching "used to": "Do I do this now?" (No) "Did I do it regularly in the past?" (Yes)

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Effective questioning is a core communication skill, not just a classroom technique. In a job interview, for example, a candidate who asks only closed questions ("Is the salary negotiable?") may come across as passive, whereas open questions ("What does success look like in this role after the first year?") signal genuine curiosity. Concept Checking Questions, though originally developed for language teaching, apply the same principle to any explanation: rather than asking "Does that make sense?" — which almost always gets an automatic "yes" — a good communicator checks understanding by asking the listener to restate or apply the idea.

  1. Effective questioning is only useful in classrooms. ___
  2. Asking only closed questions in an interview can seem passive. ___
  3. Open questions can signal curiosity. ___
  4. "Does that make sense?" reliably reveals understanding. ___
  5. Asking someone to restate an idea is a better way to check understanding. ___

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: identify the concept.
  1. "You don't really think that's a good idea, do you?" a) a leading question  b) a neutral open question
  2. A question checking HOW to do a task (not meaning) is called: a) an ICQ  b) a CCQ
  3. "So we're all clear then?" said without pausing is often: a) rhetorical, not a genuine check  b) a strong CCQ
  4. The best CCQ for "barely" (as in "I barely passed") tests: a) whether the result was close to failing  b) whether the listener is tired
  5. An open question in a formal interview is preferred over a closed one because it: a) invites a detailed, evaluative response  b) is quicker to answer
  6. A tag question used purely for social agreement ("...isn't it?") functions like: a) a closed confirmation-seeking device  b) a true open question
Exercise 15 — Improve each flawed check by writing a targeted CCQ or ICQ.
  1. To check "although" is understood: "Do you understand the conjunction?"
  2. To check students know to work in pairs: "Is everyone clear?"
  3. To check "hardly any": "Clear?"
  4. To check the time given for a task: "Enough time?"
  5. To check "as long as": "You get it?"
