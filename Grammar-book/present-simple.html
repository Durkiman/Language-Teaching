The Present Simple

+ Adverbs of Frequency

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

The present simple is built from the base form of the verb. The only change happens in the he / she / it form, which adds -s or -es.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + base verb (+ -s/-es) She works from home. / I work from home.
Negative subject + do/does + not + base verb She doesn't work on Sundays.
Question Do/Does + subject + base verb ? Does she work on weekends?
Spelling note: verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o add -es (watch → watches, go → goes); a consonant + -y changes to -ies (study → studies), but a vowel + -y just adds -s (play → plays).

When do we use it?

🔁 Habits & routines
I brush my teeth twice a day.
🌍 Facts & general truths
The Earth orbits the Sun.
🏠 Permanent situations
My parents live in Toronto.
🕐 Schedules & timetables
The train leaves at 6 p.m.
💭 Feelings & states
I understand the problem.
📖 Instructions & narration
First, you mix the eggs and sugar.

Adverbs of frequency

Adverbs of frequency say how often something happens. They usually go before the main verb, but after the verb "to be":

  • She always arrives on time. (before main verb)
  • She is always on time. (after "to be")
  • Expressions like every day, once a week, twice a month usually go at the end of the sentence: I go swimming twice a week.
⚡ Don't confuse it with the Present Continuous!
Present Simple

Habits, routines, and things that are generally true.

I read the news every morning.

Signal words: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never

Present Continuous

Actions happening right now, or temporarily around now.

I am reading the news right now.

Signal words: now, at the moment, currently, these days

Third-person -s spelling rules

RuleExamples
Most verbs: add -swork → works, eat → eats, play → plays
-s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -eswatch → watches, wash → washes, fix → fixes, go → goes
consonant + y → -iesstudy → studies, try → tries, fly → flies
vowel + y → just add -splay → plays, enjoy → enjoys
