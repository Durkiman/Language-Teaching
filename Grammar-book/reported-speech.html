Reported Speech

Statements, Questions & Commands

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Reported (indirect) speech tells us what someone said without quoting their exact words. When the reporting verb is in the past, tenses usually shift "one step back" — this is called backshifting.

Direct speech Reported speech
"I am tired," she said. She said (that) she was tired.
"I have finished," he said. He said (that) he had finished.
"Do you like coffee?" she asked. She asked if I liked coffee.
"Close the door," he said. He told me to close the door.
No backshift needed when the reporting verb is present ("She says she's tired") or the fact is still true / a general truth ("He said the earth is round").

Three sentence types

💬 Statements
said (that) + clause
❓ Questions
asked if/whether (yes/no); asked + wh-word (statement order)
📢 Commands/requests
told/asked + object + to-infinitive
⚡ Questions keep statement word order
Direct question

"Where do you live?"

Reported question

She asked where I lived.

Pronouns and time/place words change too: "I'll see you tomorrow" → he said he'd see me the next day. "Here" → there; "this" → that; "now" → then.
