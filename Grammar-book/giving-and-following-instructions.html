Giving & Following Instructions

Clear Language & ICQs

📘 1. Understanding the Skill

Clear instructions use short sentences, imperatives (Open, Write, Circle), one step at a time, and sequencing words (First..., Then..., Finally...).

🔎 What is an ICQ?

An Instruction Checking Question confirms someone understood how to do a task — not the language content (that's a CCQ). Good ICQs are short and specific: "Are you working alone or with a partner?" "How many minutes do you have?" Never ask "Do you understand the instructions?" — it's too vague to reveal real confusion.

Golden rule: instruct first, then check — never ask "Understand?" before you've even said what to do.

LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

Poorly given instructions are one of the most common causes of confusion in both classrooms and workplaces. A supervisor who says, "Just sort out the filing system sometime, whenever you get a chance," leaves too much ambiguity: what counts as "sorted," and by when? Clear instructions specify the task, the sequence, the format, and the deadline, and are followed by a specific check — not a vague "Does everyone understand?", which tends to produce silence or automatic nodding regardless of actual comprehension.

  1. Poor instructions are rare in workplaces. ___
  2. The filing example leaves the deadline unclear. ___
  3. Clear instructions specify task, sequence, format, and deadline. ___
  4. "Does everyone understand?" reliably reveals confusion. ___
  5. Vague checks often produce automatic nodding. ___

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: identify the concept.
  1. A question confirming HOW to do a task is a(n): a) ICQ  b) CCQ
  2. A question confirming understanding of a word or grammar point is a(n): a) CCQ  b) ICQ
  3. "First..., then..., finally..." is an example of: a) explicit sequencing language  b) implicit sequencing
  4. Asking "Any questions?" then immediately moving on is often: a) a redundant, low-value check  b) a thorough check
  5. Instructions with unstated deadlines commonly lead to: a) ambiguity and missed expectations  b) greater efficiency
  6. Demonstrating a task before students begin helps: a) reduce the need for lengthy verbal instructions  b) replace ICQs entirely
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.

  2. "Are you working individual or in a group?"
  3. "How much time you have for complete this?"
  4. "Please to submit the report by Friday."
  5. "Make sure everyone understanding the deadline."
