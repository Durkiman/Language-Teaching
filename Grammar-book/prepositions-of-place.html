Prepositions of Place

Describing Where Things Are

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

PrepositionUseExample
inenclosed spaces, cities/countriesthe keys are in the drawer
onsurfaces, streetsthe book is on the table
atspecific points, addresses, eventsmeet me at the entrance
under / over / above / below
The cat is under the table.
between / among
Between two things; among 3+.
behind / in front of
The desk is in front of the window.
next to / opposite / near
The park is next to the school.
Careful: "in the corner" (inside a room), but "on/at the corner" (outside, on a street). We arrive at a place (not "arrive to").

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: what does this idiomatic phrase mean?
  1. "The negotiations took place amid rising tensions." a) among/in the middle of  b) above
  2. "The village lies beneath the mountain." — "beneath" is formal for: a) under  b) between
  3. "The new office sits alongside the river." a) next to  b) opposite
  4. "Tensions simmered beneath the surface of the negotiations." a) figurative (hidden/underlying)  b) literal spatial
  5. "The committee convened within the confines of the old chamber." a) inside the limits of  b) outside
  6. "One building stands towering over the other." a) much taller than  b) next to
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.

  5. The warehouse is at the outskirts of town.
