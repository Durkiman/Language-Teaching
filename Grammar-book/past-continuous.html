The Past Continuous

vs. Past Simple

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

Formation

The past continuous is built with was/were + verb-ing. It describes an action that was in progress at a moment in the past.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative subject + was/were + verb-ing I was cooking dinner. / They were cooking dinner.
Negative subject + wasn't/weren't + verb-ing She wasn't listening.
Question Was/Were + subject + verb-ing ? Were you waiting long?
Spelling note: drop a silent -e before adding -ing (make → making); double the final consonant after one stressed vowel (stop → stopping, run → running); verbs ending in -ie change to -y (die → dying, lie → lying).

When do we use it?

⏱️ Action in progress at a past moment
At 8 p.m. last night, I was watching TV.
💥 Background action, interrupted
I was cooking when the phone rang.
🔀 Two parallel actions
She was reading while he was cooking.
🎬 Setting the scene in a story
It was raining and the wind was blowing.
🏕️ Temporary past situations
I was living in Paris that year.
😤 Annoying repeated habits (+ always)
He was always losing his keys.
⚡ Which action was longer?
Past Continuous

The longer, background action — already in progress.

I was walking home when it started to rain.

Past Simple

The shorter, interrupting action — a single completed event.

I was walking home when it started to rain.

While vs. when
while + past continuous

While I was cooking, she was setting the table.

when + past simple

I was cooking when she arrived.

📊 2. Reference Tables

-ing spelling rules

RuleExamples
Most verbs: add -ingwalk → walking, read → reading, play → playing
Silent -e: drop it, add -ingmake → making, write → writing, dance → dancing
Stressed consonant-vowel-consonant: double the consonantstop → stopping, run → running, sit → sitting
-ie → -yingdie → dying, lie → lying, tie → tying

was / were at a glance

SubjectForm
I / he / she / itwas (+ not → wasn't)
you / we / theywere (+ not → weren't)
