Demonstrative Adjectives

This, That, These, Those

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

This/that/these/those point to something specific. Choose the right word based on two things: is it one or many, and is it near or far (in space, time, or the conversation)?

this
singular, near — This book is mine.
these
plural, near — These shoes are new.
that
singular, far — That house is huge.
those
plural, far — Those mountains are beautiful.
Careful: adjective vs pronoun. This book is mine (adjective + noun). This is mine (pronoun, stands alone — no noun after it). Same four words, two different jobs.

18 exercises across all six CEFR levels — three per level, increasing in difficulty. Answers on the last page.

LEVEL A1 · Beginner
Exercise 1 — Multiple choice: circle the correct form.
  1. pen is mine. (one, near)
  2. shoes are nice. (many, far)
  3. book is interesting. (one, near)
  4. car is expensive. (one, far)
  5. apples are fresh. (many, near)
  6. mountains are tall. (many, far)
Exercise 2 — Matching: match each word (1–4) to its description (a–d).
1. this
a. plural, far
2. these
b. singular, near
3. that
c. singular, far
4. those
d. plural, near
Exercise 3 — Fill in the blank with this, that, these, or those.
  1. bag (in my hand) is heavy.
  2. birds (up in the sky) are flying south.
  3. house (across the street) is old.
  4. shoes (on my feet) are new.
  5. movie (we're watching now) is boring.
  6. people (over there) are my neighbours.
LEVEL A2 · Elementary
Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

"This coat is mine — I bought it last week. Those coats over there belong to my sisters. This week has been really busy for us. That trip we took last summer was much more relaxing. These photos on my phone are from that trip."

  1. "This coat" refers to something nearby.
  2. "Those coats" belong to the speaker.
  3. "This week" refers to the current week.
  4. "That trip" happened last summer.
  5. "These photos" are from a different trip.
Exercise 5 — Choose the correct word to complete each sentence.
  1. Can you pass me book? (the one right next to you)
  2. Look at stars! (far away, in the sky)
  3. years have been difficult. (recent, up to now)
  4. I remember day well. (a specific day in the past)
Exercise 6 — Reorder the words to make correct sentences.
  1. mine / is / book / this
  2. nice / those / are / shoes
  3. interesting / this / is / lesson
  4. expensive / are / cars / those
  5. far / house / is / too / that
LEVEL B1 · Intermediate
Exercise 7 — Find and correct the mistake in each sentence.
  1. This shoes are too small.
  2. Those car over there is mine.
  3. These book is very good.
  4. That mountains are beautiful.
  5. This people are my friends.
  6. Those pen is broken.
Exercise 8 — Complete each sentence with the correct form, paying attention to whether it's used as an adjective (+noun) or a pronoun (alone).
  1. is my favourite song. (pronoun)
  2. song is my favourite. (adjective)
  3. are the keys I lost. (pronoun, plural, near)
  4. Whose are bags? (adjective, plural, far)
Exercise 9 — Multiple choice: what does "that" express in each sentence?
  1. "That was a lovely evening."
  2. "What's that smell?"
  3. "I can't believe you did that!"
  4. "That was in 2019."
LEVEL B2 · Upper-Intermediate
Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

"I've never quite understood that attitude — the idea that hard work alone guarantees success. This is something I've thought about a lot recently, especially after that conversation we had last month. Those assumptions we grew up with don't always hold up under scrutiny, do they?"

  1. "That attitude" refers to something the speaker agrees with.
  2. "This" refers to a current, ongoing thought.
  3. "That conversation" happened recently, last month.
  4. "Those assumptions" refers to something from childhood/the past.
Exercise 11 — Find and correct the (subtler) mistake in each sentence.
  1. Those kind of people annoy me. (singular agreement)
  2. This sort of things happen all the time.
  3. I don't like them kind of movies.
  4. That there house belongs to my uncle.
Exercise 12 — Guided writing.

Write 6–8 sentences comparing something from your past to your present situation, using this/these for now and that/those for the past, at least twice each.

LEVEL C1 · Advanced
Exercise 13 — Rewrite each sentence, replacing the vague reference with a precise demonstrative + noun.
  1. It happened, and it changed everything. (that event)
  2. They exist, and they're the reason for the delay. (those factors)
  3. It's happening, and it worries me. (this trend)
  4. It was said, and I still disagree with it. (that comment)
Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: rhetorical/discourse use of "this/that".
  1. "This brings us to the next point."
  2. "That said, there are exceptions."
  3. "This is not to say it's simple."
  4. "With that in mind, let's proceed."
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.
  1. That said, this don't change the outcome.
  2. This brings us onto next point.
  3. With that being said in mind, we proceeded.
  4. Those kind of assumptions is rarely accurate.
  5. This is not for say it's straightforward.
LEVEL C2 · Proficiency
Exercise 16 — Open writing.

In 80–120 words, write a formal discussion passage using this/that as discourse markers (this brings us to, that said, with that in mind) to organise your argument, not just to point at objects.

Exercise 17 — Reorder into formal, complex discourse sentences.
  1. next / brings / point / this / the / to / us
  2. said / exceptions / that / are / there
  3. proceed / mind / with / let's / that / in
  4. simple / this / say / not / to / it's / is
  5. accurate / kind / rarely / that / assumptions / of / are
Exercise 18 — Correct the nuanced errors.
  1. This bring us to the next point.
  2. That being said, this remain a concern.
  3. With that in mind, we was able to proceed.
  4. Those kind of claim is misleading.
  5. This is not for to say it's easy.
