Relative Clauses

Defining & Non-Defining

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

A relative clause adds information about a noun using a relative pronoun (who, which, that, whose, where, when). Defining clauses identify which noun we mean; non-defining clauses add extra, non-essential information.

Type Feature Example
Defining no commas; can use "that"; pronoun sometimes omittable The man who called is my uncle.
Non-defining set off by commas; never "that"; never omitted My uncle, who lives in Peru, called.
Omitting the pronoun: in a defining clause, you can drop who/which/that only when it is the object of the clause — "The book (that) I read" — never when it is the subject: "The man who called" (cannot drop "who").

Relative pronouns at a glance

👤 who / that
for people: the woman who/that teaches us.
📦 which / that
for things: the book which/that I bought.
🏷️ whose
possession: the man whose car was stolen.
📍 where
place: the café where we met.
🕐 when
time: the year when I was born.
💭 which (whole clause)
commenting on a whole idea: He passed, which pleased everyone.
⚡ Defining or non-defining?
Defining

Students who study hard pass the exam. (only those students)

Non-defining

My students, who study hard, passed the exam. (all of them, extra info)

Prepositions: in formal style the preposition moves before the pronoun ("the person to whom I spoke"); in everyday speech it moves to the end ("the person who I spoke to").

Exercise 14 — Multiple choice: what does the relative clause add here?
  1. "The proposal, which was rejected, cost months of work." → a) essential identification  b) extra commentary on the whole proposal
  2. "Employees who arrive late will be warned." → a) refers only to those employees  b) refers to all employees
  3. "The CEO, whose decision surprised everyone, resigned." → a) defining  b) non-defining
  4. "The findings, which contradicted expectations, were published." → a) identifies which findings  b) comments on all the findings
  5. "The one strategy that worked was outsourcing." → a) defining, singles it out  b) non-defining, extra detail
  6. "He lost the case, which was unexpected." → a) refers to the whole preceding clause  b) refers only to "the case"
Exercise 15 — Correct the mistakes in these complex sentences.

  3. The market, which conditions had deteriorated, forced a policy change.
  4. The candidate whom was hired lacked the necessary experience.
  5. The findings which the report was based were later disputed.
