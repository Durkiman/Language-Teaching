Articles

A / An / The / No Article

📘 1. Understanding the Rule

English has two kinds of articles: the indefinite article (a/an) for something non-specific, and the definite article (the) for something specific or already known. Sometimes no article is needed at all.

Article Use Example
a / an one of many, first mention, non-specific I saw a dog. She is an engineer.
the specific, already mentioned, unique, both speakers know it The dog barked. The sun is bright.
no article (∅) plurals/uncountables in general, most proper nouns I like ∅ dogs. ∅ Paris is beautiful.
A vs. an: choose by the sound, not the letter — "a university" (sounds like "yoo"), "an hour" (silent h), "a European" but "an umbrella".

When do we use "the"?

🔁 Second mention
I saw a cat. The cat was black.
🌍 Unique things
The moon, the internet, the world.
📍 Made specific by context
Close the door (there's only one).
🎻 Superlatives & ordinals
The best day, the first time.
🎸 Musical instruments, cinema/theatre
She plays the piano. Let's go to the cinema.
🏞️ Rivers, seas, mountain ranges, plural country names
The Nile, the Alps, the Netherlands.
No article with most proper nouns (Mark, France, Mount Everest), meals in general (I had breakfast), languages/subjects (she speaks Spanish), and general plural/uncountable nouns (Books are expensive).

LEVEL A2 · Elementary
Exercise 4 — True or false? Read, then mark T or F.

Yesterday I bought a book and an umbrella. The book was about the history of Japan, and I read it on the train. The umbrella was blue, and I forgot it at the station! I usually don't like reading on trains, but this book was so interesting that I didn't notice the time passing.

  1. The writer bought a book and an umbrella.
  2. The book was about France.
  3. The umbrella was blue.
  4. The writer forgot the book at the station.
  5. The writer found the book interesting.

Exercise 10 — Read, then mark each statement T or F.

An earlier study had reached similar conclusions, but this was the first to examine the effect across different age groups.

  1. The research team is based in France.
  2. The study involved over two hundred volunteers.
  3. A lack of sleep improved recall ability.
