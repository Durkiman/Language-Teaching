Budget review — a short exchange

Sana: Before we move to the next item on the agenda, I want to flag a concern about the marketing budget.

Karim: Sure, go ahead.

Sana: I think we're allocating too much to paid ads and not enough to —

Fayçal: Sorry, can I just come in here? We already discussed this with the stakeholders last month.

Sana: Hang on, let me finish — I know we discussed it, but the numbers have changed since then.

Fayçal: Fair enough, go on.

Sana: Thanks. I'd suggest we table this item and follow up once finance has signed off on the new figures.

Karim: Agreed. I'll send everyone a recap and the action items after the call.

Key vocabulary — tap to reveal

The phrases only sound natural because of the softening underneath. Four small patterns carry the whole skill.

1 · Interrupting politely

Could / Can I just + verb...?  ·  Sorry, can I + verb...?

"Could I just come in here for a second?"

"Sorry, can I add something to that?"

Fr : Est-ce que je peux juste... / Désolé, je peux ajouter quelque chose ?

❌ "I want to say something." — too abrupt for a meeting register.
✅ "Could I just come in here?"

2 · Holding the floor

Hang on, let me + verb.  ·  If I could just + verb...

"Hang on, let me finish this point."

"If I could just finish, then I'll pass it over to you."

Fr : Attends, laisse-moi finir. / Si je pouvais juste terminer...

❌ "Wait wait wait." — reads as impatient in a professional setting.
✅ "Hang on, let me finish."

3 · Yielding the floor

Go on.  ·  Sorry, you were saying?

"Go on, please."

"Sorry — you were saying? Please continue."

Fr : Vas-y. / Désolé, tu disais ?

❌ Staying silent and letting it feel awkward.
✅ Actively inviting them back in signals confidence, not weakness.

4 · Softening a request or question

Would you mind if I...?  ·  I was wondering whether we could...

"Would you mind if I asked a quick question?"

"I was wondering whether we could revisit the timeline."

Fr : Ça vous dérangerait si... / Je me demandais si on pourrait...

❌ "I need you to explain that again." — too direct for most meeting contexts.
✅ "Would you mind explaining that again?"
